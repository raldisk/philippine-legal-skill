# Land Registration

## Primary Sources
**P.D. 1529 (Property Registration Decree)** — judicial and administrative registration. **Commonwealth Act 141 (Public Land Act)** — disposition of alienable and disposable (A&D) public land. **LRA Rules (A.M. No. 12-11-2-SC)** — procedural rules. **DENR** — land classification; CENRO/PENRO certifications.

---

## Public Domain Classification
**Constitutional classification (Art. XII §3)**: All lands of the public domain classified as: (1) agricultural; (2) forest/timber; (3) mineral lands; (4) national parks. **Only agricultural lands may be alienated.** Forest, mineral, and national park lands are inalienable — cannot be registered regardless of possession duration.

**Alienable and Disposable (A&D) land**: Public agricultural land released by the DENR for private disposition. Proof required: CENRO or PENRO certification + DENR-approved land classification map showing the area is within the A&D zone.

***Republic v. Malabanan* (G.R. No. 179987, Sept. 3, 2009, En Banc)**: The land must be A&D not only at the time of application but also at the time the possession commenced. Possession of land that was forest/mineral land during the possession period does not qualify — even if the land was later reclassified as A&D.

---

## Modes of Original Registration
**§14(1) P.D. 1529 — Judicial confirmation**:
- Open, continuous, exclusive, and notorious (OCEN) possession.
- Under bona fide claim of ownership.
- Since **June 12, 1945 or earlier**.
- Land must be A&D at time of application.
- Possession of predecessors-in-interest may be tacked.

**§14(2) P.D. 1529 — Acquisition by prescription**: Private lands only; acquisitive prescription under the Civil Code.

**Free Patent (C.A. 141 §§44–45)**: Filipino citizens who have continuously occupied and cultivated A&D agricultural land for the required period may apply to the DENR for a free patent. Free patent land: **inalienable for 5 years** from date of patent. After alienation: free patent lands issued to members of national cultural minorities are subject to longer restrictions.

**Homestead Patent (C.A. 141 §§12–22)**: Heads of family; cultivate and improve within the required period; inalienability restriction: **5 years** from date of patent.

**Sales Patent**: Awarded by public auction; inalienability restriction applies.

## Judicial Registration Procedure
1. File application with **RTC (acting as Land Registration Court)** in the province/city where land is located.
2. **Publication**: once in Official Gazette + newspaper of general circulation; posting on land and in court.
3. Survey and plan: approved by LRA; must be duly executed by licensed geodetic engineer.
4. Hearing; muniments of title presented (tax declarations, deeds, possession history, survey, witnesses).
5. **Order of general default** (§26 P.D. 1529) — against the whole world.
6. Judgment; **Decree of Registration** issued by LRA; **OCT** issued by Registry of Deeds.

---

## Transfer — Subsequent Registration
**Deeds**: In writing, notarized, registered with Registry of Deeds. Foreign-language instruments: certified translation required.

**Priority of registration**: Earlier registration prevails over later registration. Registered instrument prevails over unregistered instrument even if latter is prior in time.

**Annotations on title**: Mortgages; adverse claims (30-day expiry, renewable); lis pendens; levy on execution — all registered by annotation; constitute constructive notice.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Land Registration
After *Malabanan*, the land must be A&D at the time possession began AND at the time of application — possession of formerly forest/mineral land does not count even if it is now A&D. The §14(1) anchor date is June 12, 1945 — not "time immemorial." Free patent and homestead patent lands are inalienable for 5 years — sales within that period are void. The CENRO/PENRO certification AND the DENR land classification map are both required to prove A&D status — one without the other is insufficient after *Malabanan*. Priority of registration applies to voluntary dealings — involuntary dealings (like attachment) follow different priority rules based on when the lien attaches.

## Original Registration in Detail

**Jurisdictional requirements**: Application filed in the province/city where land is located; if different regions: file where the greater portion lies. LRC (Land Registration Court = RTC) has exclusive jurisdiction over land registration proceedings.

**Survey and technical description**: Government survey or private survey approved by LRA. Technical description must be verified and approved; geodetic engineer certified. Inconsistencies between title and survey — survey prevails for technical boundary; title prevails for identity of owner.

**Notice to adjoining owners**: Adjoining lot owners must be specifically notified of the application; their non-appearance triggers the default order against them; they remain bound by the decree.

**Publication**: Done to bind the whole world; defective publication = court acquires no jurisdiction = registration void even if parties appeared. *Republic v. Nillas* (GR 159598): lack of proper publication deprives LRC of jurisdiction.

---

## Administrative Registration (DENR/LMB Processes)

**Free patent — current procedure**: File with CENRO (Community Environment and Natural Resources Office); CENRO inspects; recommends to PENRO (Provincial Environment); DENR Secretary approves; patent issued. LRA transmits to Registry of Deeds for OCT issuance.

**IPRA lands (R.A. 8371 — Indigenous Peoples Rights Act)**: Ancestral domains: defined and delineated by NCIP (National Commission on Indigenous Peoples); Certificate of Ancestral Domain Title (CADT) issued; not covered by Torrens system; inalienable; belongs to indigenous cultural community collectively. Ancestral lands: individual members' titled lands within ancestral domain; Certificate of Ancestral Land Title (CALT).

**RA 11573 (Confirmation of Imperfect Titles, 2021)**: Amended §14(1) PD 1529 — for judicial confirmation of imperfect title, applicant now needs ONLY to show the land is A&D AND has 20 years of open, continuous, exclusive possession. Removed the requirement of proving possession since June 12, 1945 for future applications (transitional provisions still apply for pending cases).

---

## Title Defects and Nullity

**Void titles**: Issued over inalienable/non-disposable land (forest, mineral, national park); issued to persons disqualified under the Constitution (e.g., foreign nationals — except by hereditary succession); obtained through forgery; issued by a court without jurisdiction. Void title passes no right regardless of subsequent transfers.

**Voidable titles**: Obtained through fraud but the court had jurisdiction and land was A&D — voidable; susceptible to reconveyance if innocent purchaser has not yet acquired.

**Double titling**: If two certificates cover the same land, the earlier title prevails. *Aboitiz v. Court of Appeals* (GR 103567): Earlier registered title defeats later title regardless of possession.

---

## Assurance Fund (§§95–97 P.D. 1529)

**Purpose**: Compensates parties injured by wrongful registration when remedy against the wrongdoer is unavailable (e.g., wrongdoer insolvent, dead, unknown). Fund maintained by LRA from registration fees.

**Claim conditions**: Person must have suffered loss/damage from the operation of the Torrens Act; not through own negligence or fraud; no remedy available against wrongdoer. Claim filed with the National Treasurer; prescribes in 6 years from loss.

**Amount of recovery**: Value of the land at the time of loss. Not available for mental anguish or other non-property damages.

## Key Cases — Land Registration

**Republic v. Malabanan** (GR 179987, 2009, En Banc): Landmark ruling; land must be classified as A&D before possession can start the §14(1) prescription clock; reclassification from forest to A&D does not retroactively validate earlier possession.

**RA 11573** (2021) legislative response to *Malabanan*: Modified §14(1) P.D. 1529 by removing the June 12, 1945 anchor date requirement for future applications; now requires only 20-year OCEN possession of A&D land.

**Heirs of Mario Malabanan v. Republic** (GR 179987, 2013, MR): Affirmed the 2009 ruling but clarified that prescription under §14(2) NCC can cover private lands (already titled or no longer public land); not a basis for original registration of public A&D lands.

**Director of Lands v. CA** (GR L-58867): Mere tax declarations do not prove ownership of public land; they are only evidence of possession claim.

**Republic v. Espinosa** (GR 171514): Without a CENRO/PENRO certification AND a certified DENR land classification map, A&D status is not proven; court cannot rely on self-serving tax declarations.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Land Registration Update
R.A. 11573 (2021) changed the §14(1) anchor date — always check whether the application was pending before or after R.A. 11573's effectivity; transitional rules matter. Both CENRO/PENRO certification AND the DENR land classification map are required — one without the other is insufficient. Void titles (issued over inalienable land) cannot be validated by any amount of time or possession — even by an IPV. Assurance Fund claims have a 6-year prescriptive period — failure to file within 6 years forecloses recovery. Double titling resolved by priority of registration date, not priority of possession. IPRA ancestral domain is NOT Torrens-system property — completely separate legal regime governed by NCIP; never apply P.D. 1529 principles to CADT/CALT.

---

## Administrative Reconstitution of Titles

**RA 26 and LRA Administrative Reconstitution**: Lost or destroyed titles may be administratively reconstituted if the loss/destruction was due to fire, flood, or other force majeure and there is sufficient basis (owner's duplicate, co-owner's duplicate, certified copy from RD, deed on file with RD, tax records).

**Judicial reconstitution**: Filed with RTC; required where administrative reconstitution is unavailable or contested; same publication requirements as original registration.

**Fraudulent reconstitution**: Courts have voided fraudulently reconstituted titles; subsequent purchasers of fraudulently reconstituted titles — may or may not be IPV depending on whether they had actual notice of the fraud or suspicious circumstances.

---

## Cadastral Survey and Cadastral Registration

**Cadastral proceedings (C.A. 141 §§34–38; R.A. 2259)**: Government-initiated survey of all lands in a municipality/city; all land owners required to file answers declaring their claims; default order against those who fail to appear; after hearing, court decrees registration to deserving claimants.

**Effect of cadastral registration**: Same as voluntary registration under P.D. 1529; title is indefeasible after 1 year from decree.

**Unclaimed lands in cadastral**: Lands in cadastral areas with no claimant — declared public domain; remain alienable and disposable if classified as such by DENR.

---

## Section 48(b) of CA 141 — Historical and Current Application

**Historical rule**: Section 48(b) of CA 141 as originally worded: imperfect title applicants must have possessed since July 26, 1894. Changed over time; amended to June 12, 1945; interpreted by *Malabanan* (2009) to require the land to be A&D before possession started.

**R.A. 11573 (2021) amendment**: The 20-year OCEN possession is now sufficient for §14(1) applications; land must be classified A&D by DENR with certification. The old June 12, 1945 anchor date applies only to pending applications at the time R.A. 11573 took effect.

**Tacking of possession**: Successor-in-interest may tack the possession of his predecessor for purposes of computing the required OCEN period; requires proof of lawful succession (deed of sale, inheritance, etc.).

## Practical Notes on Land Acquisition

**Due diligence checklist for buyers**:
1. Obtain certified true copy of title from Registry of Deeds (not from seller).
2. Check for annotations: mortgages, adverse claims, lis pendens, encumbrances.
3. Check if title is within A&D area (for unregistered land) via CENRO/PENRO.
4. Verify consistency between the technical description on title and the survey plan.
5. Physically inspect the property; identify actual occupants.
6. Inquire from adjoining landowners about boundary disputes.
7. Obtain tax clearance from LGU; verify if RPT is current.
8. Check zoning classification with LGU planning office.
9. Verify seller's identity and authority (if through attorney-in-fact, verify SPA).
10. Secure BIR eCAR and pay applicable taxes before RD registration.

**Red flags**: Title is "reconstituted"; title has multiple or successive TCTs within short periods; title is in name of a deceased person; seller is not in possession; multiple claimants; land is within known agrarian reform area; property is in flood-prone or protected area.

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Land Registration Consolidated
Never cite June 12, 1945 as the anchor date for applications filed after R.A. 11573 (2021) took effect — check which law applies to the application. Both CENRO certification AND land classification map are required to prove A&D status — neither alone is sufficient. Tacking of possession requires proof of chain of title/succession — mere claim of tacking insufficient. Spurious reconstituted titles do not protect IPVs; always distinguish fraudulent reconstitution from fraudulent original registration (different legal consequences). Tax declarations are NOT proof of ownership of public land — they are only indicia of claim of possession. Section 48(b) CA 141 and §14(1) PD 1529 are the same in substance; they both govern imperfect title confirmation; the current rule is 20-year OCEN plus A&D certification.

## Summary Reference — Original Registration

| Mode | Legal Basis | Key Requirement | Inalienability |
|---|---|---|---|
| Judicial confirmation (§14[1] PD 1529) | PD 1529; CA 141 §48(b) as amended | 20 years OCEN; A&D land with CENRO cert + DENR map | None after OCT |
| Free Patent | CA 141 §§44–45 | Continuous cultivation; occupancy requirements | 5 years from patent |
| Homestead Patent | CA 141 §§12–22 | Cultivation and improvement | 5 years from patent |
| Sales Patent | CA 141 public auction | Payment of purchase price | Per patent terms |
| Cadastral | CA 141 §§34–38 | File answer to cadastral petition | None after decree |

**Proof required for §14(1)**:
1. CENRO/PENRO certification — land is within alienable and disposable area.
2. Certified copy of DENR land classification map — shows area is classified A&D.
3. Testimony of witnesses to OCEN possession.
4. Tax declarations and receipts for 20-year period.
5. Approved survey plan (geodetic engineer-certified; LRA-verified).
6. Muniments of title (deeds from predecessor if possession is tacked).

**Effect of annotation of adverse claim**: Adverse claim annotated on TCT — binds the registered owner and all persons dealing with the property for the 30-day effective period. A buyer who purchases during this period takes subject to the adverse claimant's right if the adverse claimant succeeds in court. Failure to file an action within 30 days does not automatically mean the adverse claim is without merit — it only loses its annotated binding effect unless renewed.
