# Agrarian Reform

## Primary Sources
**Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Law (R.A. 6657, 1988) as amended by CARPER (R.A. 9700, 2009)** — CARP land redistribution. **Department of Agrarian Reform (DAR)** — primary implementing agency; issues Administrative Orders. **Land Bank of the Philippines (LBP)** — disburses just compensation.

---

## CARP Coverage
**Covered**: All public and private agricultural lands regardless of commodity produced, including idle and abandoned land. Agricultural land = land devoted to or suitable for agriculture.

**Retention limit**: Landowner may retain up to **5 hectares** of agricultural land. **Children of landowner** ≥ 15 years old actually tilling or directly managing farm may each retain **3 hectares**.

**Exemptions from coverage**:
- Residential, commercial, industrial, institutional land.
- Land for government use.
- Land currently planted with lawful crops other than rice/corn with existing ≤5 ha holdings.
- Private lands voluntarily offered by owner who is not a corporation → covered by separate voluntary procedures.
- P.D. 27 lands (rice and corn, previously covered under Tenants Emancipation Decree).

**Land use conversion**: Agricultural land cannot be converted to non-agricultural use without **DAR approval**. Unauthorized conversion is illegal and may result in cancellation and criminal liability.

---

## Land Acquisition and Distribution
**Compulsory acquisition**: DAR acquires covered land; process: identification → notice to landowner → offer of compensation → landowner reply → adjudication if no agreement.

**Voluntary offer to sell (VOS)**: Landowner may voluntarily offer; receives price incentive.

**Just compensation**: Must reflect the value at the time of taking, with legal interest thereafter. *Land Bank v. Court of Appeals*: just compensation must be real, substantial, full, and ample compensation.

Factors under §17 R.A. 6657: cost of acquisition; current value of like properties; nature, actual use and income; sworn valuation by owner; assessments; government valuation at time of taking; social and economic benefits.

**LBP role**: Issues cash and agrarian reform bonds (ARBs) as compensation components. LBP initial valuation is subject to contest by the landowner.

**Agrarian Reform Beneficiaries (ARBs)**: Priority: (1) agricultural lessees; (2) regular farmworkers; (3) seasonal farmworkers; (4) other farmworkers; (5) actual tillers; (6) collectives/cooperatives.

---

## Tenancy and Leasehold
**Conversion from share tenancy**: Under CARL, share tenancy is prohibited; tenants automatically converted to agricultural lessees.

**Leasehold rental**: 25% of the average normal harvest of the three preceding crop years (for rice/corn, P.D. 27; for CARL leasehold, DAR governs).

**Security of tenure**: Agricultural lessee cannot be ejected except for grounds under the Agricultural Land Reform Code (R.A. 3844): non-payment of lease rentals; substantial failure to comply with terms; abandonment; conversion authorized by DAR.

---

## Jurisdiction
**DARAB (DAR Adjudication Board)**: Primary quasi-judicial jurisdiction over agrarian disputes, including: CARP implementation; tenancy relationships; disturbance compensation; emancipation patent issues.

**Special Agrarian Courts (SACs)**: Designated RTCs with exclusive original jurisdiction over (1) **just compensation** disputes and (2) criminal violations of R.A. 6657. SAC, NOT DARAB, determines final just compensation.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Agrarian Reform
Never state that the CARP coverage threshold is 5 hectares — the 5-hectare figure is the **retention limit**, not a minimum coverage area; land of any size may be covered if it is agricultural land. DAR approval is required for land use conversion — unauthorized conversion is a violation subject to cancellation and penalties. Just compensation in agrarian cases is a judicial determination by the SAC — the DARAB does not have jurisdiction over just compensation; never advise that DARAB's valuation is final. The LBP initial valuation is the starting point — landowners may contest it before the SAC. Security of tenure of agricultural lessees is not absolute — the specific grounds for ejectment under R.A. 3844 apply.
---

## Just Compensation — Expanded Analysis

**Constitutional requirement (Art. III §9)**: Private property shall not be taken for public use without just compensation. In agrarian cases, the State compulsorily acquires agricultural land — this is a taking requiring just compensation.

**LBP formula and factors (§17 R.A. 6657)**: Compensation based on: cost of acquisition; current value of like properties; nature, actual use, and income; owner's sworn valuation; tax assessment; government assessment; social and economic benefits of the property; non-payment of taxes/monetary obligations.

**DAR-LBP Administrative Order No. 7, Series of 2011 (AO No. 07-11) formula**: LBP follows a formula using Capitalized Net Income (CNI), Comparable Sales (CS), and Market Data Approach (MDA), weighted per formula. Courts must determine just compensation independently — not rubber-stamp LBP formula.

**Judicial review of just compensation**: SAC must conduct a full trial; cannot simply confirm LBP valuation. *LBP v. Spouses Banal* (GR 167895): SAC has independent authority to determine just compensation; LBP's valuation is merely a starting point.

**Interest on delayed payment**: Landowners entitled to legal interest (12% per annum historically; now 6% under *Nacar* jurisprudence from July 2013 onwards) on just compensation from taking until full payment. *LBP v. Orilla* (GR 213169): interest runs from time of taking, not from filing.

## CARP Coverage — Additional Details

**Sugarcane lands**: Previously exempt under "exemption for land already tenanted under 25-year lease agreements"; CARPER (R.A. 9700) removed most exemptions; sugar lands now covered.

**Livestock and poultry lands**: Supreme Court in *Luz Farms v. Secretary of Agrarian Reform* (GR 86889, 1990): lands devoted primarily to livestock, swine, or poultry are NOT agricultural lands for CARP purposes — they are industrial lands; CARP cannot cover them.

**Five-hectare retention limit**: Landowner with multiple parcels may consolidate the 5-hectare retention to one parcel; or distribute the retention among multiple parcels as long as total does not exceed 5 hectares. Children's 3-hectare retention: children must be ≥ 15 years old AND actually tilling or directly managing at time of coverage; absentee children do not qualify.

---

## ARB Rights and Amortization

**Emancipation patents and CLOAs**: Documents of title given to ARBs upon completion of CARP process. Emancipation Patent (EP) — for PD 27 lands. Certificate of Land Ownership Award (CLOA) — for R.A. 6657 lands.

**Amortization**: ARBs pay the government for the land in installments (amortization period under CARP: 30 years). Non-payment consequences: DAR may redistribute the land to other qualified ARBs after notice and due process.

**Inalienability of awarded land**: CLOAs and EPs may not be transferred, sold, or encumbered within **10 years** from issuance (R.A. 6657 §27). After 10 years: still subject to right of first refusal by government/other ARBs. Violation: transfer is void; land reverts.

---

## Land Use Conversion

**DAR Administrative Order on Conversion**: LGU reclassification alone does not convert agricultural land — DAR approval for conversion from agricultural to non-agricultural use is still required for CARP-covered land.

**Exemption from coverage (DAR MC 1, series of 2012)**: Land with an existing use that is not agricultural as of June 15, 1988 (CARL effectivity) is not agricultural land under CARP; landowner must secure a DAR exemption order.

**Grounds for conversion approval**: Land no longer suitable for agriculture; certification by DAR that land is not viable for agricultural purposes; conversion is for a higher and better use as determined by the DAR and local zoning ordinances.

**Unauthorized conversion — penalties**: Criminal penalties; fines; restoration of land to agricultural use; reversion to CARP coverage.

---

## DARAB Jurisdiction — Expanded

**Intra-CARP disputes**: DARAB has primary jurisdiction over: (1) rights and obligations of beneficiaries under agrarian reform program; (2) cancellation and issuance of CLOAs/EPs; (3) protest/opposition to CARP coverage; (4) determination of tenancy relationship and rights.

**NOT within DARAB jurisdiction**: Just compensation (SAC exclusive); disputes that are purely civil in nature unrelated to CARP; criminal violations of CARP (SAC/regular courts).

**DARAB hierarchy**: Adjudicator → DARAB Central Office (Provincial Agrarian Reform Adjudicator/PARAD → Regional → National) → CA (Rule 43 appeal) → SC.

---

## Key Cases — Agrarian Reform

**Association of Small Landowners in the Philippines v. Secretary of Agrarian Reform** (GR 78742, 1989): CARP is a valid exercise of police power and eminent domain; legislative classification of agricultural land for redistribution is valid; just compensation must be real, substantial, full, ample.

**Luz Farms v. Secretary of Agrarian Reform** (GR 86889, 1990): Livestock, swine, and poultry lands are NOT agricultural for CARP — excluded from coverage; agriculture contemplates farming, not animal raising for business.

**LBP v. Wycoco** (GR 140160, 2003): LBP valuation is not binding; courts must independently determine just compensation; RTC acting as SAC cannot mechanically apply the DAR-LBP formula.

**DAR v. Sutton** (GR 162070, 2005): Private lands used for livestock are exempt from CARP — reaffirms *Luz Farms* and extends to private commercial livestock operations.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Agrarian Reform Update
10-year inalienability of CLOAs/EPs means transfers within this period are void — void, not merely voidable. After 10 years, there is still a right of first refusal by the government and other ARBs — free transferability is not absolute. Just compensation interest runs from the taking, not from the DAR order or complaint. LBP formula (AO 07-11) is a starting point — SAC must exercise independent judgment. Livestock lands exempt from CARP — but the land must be actually devoted to livestock, not merely classified. DAR approval for land use conversion is required even if the LGU has already reclassified the land for non-agricultural use.

---

## Agrarian Justice — Support Services for ARBs

**Support services (§37 R.A. 6657)**: DAR and other government agencies must provide ARBs with: land surveys and titling; irrigation services; technical assistance and training; livelihood support; marketing and credit facilities. Without support services, CARP distribution alone does not ensure ARB viability.

**Agrarian Reform Infrastructure Support Project (ARISP)**: Government-funded program providing irrigation, farm-to-market roads, and post-harvest facilities to CARP beneficiaries; implemented by DA and DAR.

**Cooperative development**: DAR encourages formation of farmer cooperatives to manage distributed lands collectively; collective CLOAs issued to cooperatives where individual titling is not feasible; cooperative has juridical personality; governed by Cooperative Code (R.A. 9520).

---

## CARP and Land Taxes

**CARP lands and RPT**: Agricultural lands under CARP subject to RPT at agricultural assessment level (lower assessment level than commercial/industrial). ARBs may apply for tax exemption under local ordinances for small-scale farming.

**Income tax exemption for ARBs**: Individual ARBs who are actual farmers generally exempt from income tax on income from small-scale agricultural activities under the minimum wage exemption; cooperatives — exempt from income tax under R.A. 9520 if at least 50% of gross income from transactions with members.

---

## Agrarian Reform Program — Status Post-CARPER

**CARPER completion (2014 deadline)**: R.A. 9700 set August 30, 2014 as the deadline for completion of CARP land acquisition and distribution. Despite the deadline, significant areas remain undistributed due to legal disputes, surveys, and administrative backlog.

**Post-2014 status**: DAR continues to process pending cases; CLOA cancellations and re-awards ongoing; agrarian disputes from pre-2014 distributions remain active in DARAB and courts.

**Land conversion post-CARPER**: CARP-covered land that was improperly converted before DAR approval — subject to reversion; landowners face criminal and administrative liability.

---

## Agrarian Dispute Resolution — Practical Notes

**Disturbance compensation (§36[1] R.A. 3844)**: Tenant who agrees to leave the tenanted land — entitled to disturbance compensation of 5 years' rental at the leasehold rental rate (or 3 years for tenant who leaves voluntarily and cultivates land elsewhere). Landowner must pay before tenant actually leaves.

**Preliminary injunction in DARAB**: DARAB may issue writs of preliminary injunction and restraining orders in agrarian disputes; subject to DAR Secretary appeal.

**Criminal violations of CARP**: Unauthorized conversion, illegal transfer, non-compliance with CARP obligations — filed in SAC (designated RTC); DAR may refer criminal cases to DOJ.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Agrarian Reform Consolidated
Do not confuse DARAB jurisdiction (agrarian disputes) with SAC jurisdiction (just compensation; criminal violations) — these are separate and distinct. The 10-year inalienability of CLOAs and EPs is absolute — transfers within this period are void ab initio regardless of consideration. After 10 years, there remains a right of first refusal by government/other ARBs — this continues beyond the 10-year period. CARP retention limit is 5 hectares per landowner — NOT per family; children's retention is additional and requires actual tilling/management. Livestock lands exempt from CARP — but the exemption must be applied for; mere character of the land does not automatically exclude; DAR administrative process is required for exemption order. Disturbance compensation is 5 years of rental — not 5 years of profit from the land; the rental rate is the leasehold rental agreed or set by DAR.

**CARP phases and deadlines**:
- CARL: enacted 1988 (R.A. 6657).
- Revised/extended: CARPER R.A. 9700 (2009) — 5-year extension to 2014.
- Post-2014: DAR continues implementing pending acquisition and distribution cases under the same statutory framework; new coverage is not initiated.

**Just compensation — current formula application**: LBP uses a weighted formula combining CNI (income approach), CS (comparable sales), and MDA (market data) per DAR-LBP Joint Memorandum Circular. Courts applying §17 R.A. 6657 may depart from the formula if it does not reflect true FMV — judicial determination is paramount. Interest: *LBP v. Natividad* (GR 127198) set 12% from taking to full payment; post-*Nacar* (GR 189871, 2013) jurisprudence applies 12% p.a. before July 1, 2013 then 6% p.a. after until satisfaction. Verify the current applicable interest rate based on when taking occurred and when payment is made.

**Agrarian reform bonds**: Compensation paid to landowners includes a cash component and agrarian reform bonds (ARBs). ARBs are government securities; interest-bearing; negotiable; may be used to pay taxes, land acquisition, and government financial obligations. Face value of bonds was subject to discount in the market — landmark issues of fairness in CARP compensation. Landowners who received ARBs at below-market value and subsequently experienced inflation — raised constitutional due process and just compensation challenges addressed in *Association of Small Landowners* and subsequent cases.

**Land valuation for RPT and CARP**: Local assessor's fair market value and BIR zonal value are the two benchmarks used throughout property law — for RPT assessment, estate tax, CGT, and CARP just compensation calculations. The higher of the two is used for tax purposes (BIR). For CARP, the SAC determines just compensation independently using the §17 R.A. 6657 factors, which may exceed or fall below both benchmarks depending on the evidence. Never assume the BIR zonal value or assessor's FMV is the definitive ceiling or floor for CARP just compensation.
