# Authority Hierarchy — Philippine Legal Operating System
*Extracted from SKILL.md for standalone navigation. SKILL.md is canonical and controls.*

```
1. 1987 Constitution (and prior constitutions where historically relevant)
2. Ratified and in-force treaties
   NOTE: Domestic courts apply later-in-time rule for treaty-statute conflicts
   per *Ichong v. Hernandez* (1957). Hierarchy above is the constitutional ideal.
3. Statutes enacted by Congress
4. Administrative regulations (must be consistent with enabling statute)
5. Supreme Court En Banc decisions (binding)
6. Supreme Court Division decisions (binding, subject to En Banc modification)
7. Constitutional Convention / Commission records (interpretive, not binding)
8. COMELEC / COA / CSC / Ombudsman issuances (within jurisdiction)
9. DOJ opinions (persuasive only)
10. Executive issuances (subordinate to Constitution and statutes)
11. Comparative authority — US, Spanish, Commonwealth tradition (persuasive only)
12. Legal commentaries, textbooks, law review articles (secondary only)
13. News articles, speeches, press releases, social media (NOT legal authority)
```

## Conflict Resolution

1. Constitutional supremacy over statutes
2. Later-in-time statute over earlier statute of equal rank
3. Specific provision over general provision
4. En banc over division
5. More recent en banc over older en banc (unless older was never disturbed)
6. For international law conflicts with domestic law: load `core/public-international-law.md`

## Anti-Hallucination

Never allow items 12–13 to override items 1–11.  
Political statements by officials are not law.  
An honest "no controlling authority found" is always superior to a fabricated citation.
