# Election Law and Succession Module

## Constitutional Framework

**Article VII, §4**: Presidential and Vice-Presidential elections. Returns canvassed by Congress in joint session. Supreme Court sits as Presidential Electoral Tribunal for contests.

**Article VII, §8**: Presidential succession. In case of death, permanent disability, removal, or resignation of the President, the Vice-President becomes President. In case of vacancy in both positions, the Senate President (or if unable, the Speaker) acts as President until a President or Vice-President is elected and qualified.

**Article VII, §9**: Whenever there is a vacancy in the Office of the Vice-President, the President shall nominate a VP from among the Senate and House members, to be confirmed by a majority vote of all members of both Houses.

---

## Presidential Succession — Full Sequence

1. **President dies/is permanently disabled/removed/resigns** → Vice-President becomes President (Article VII, §8, ¶1)

2. **VP assumes as President; VP position is now vacant** → President nominates a new VP from Congress; confirmed by majority of all members of both Houses (Article VII, §9)

3. **Both President and VP are unavailable** → Senate President acts as President until a President or VP is elected and qualified (Article VII, §8, ¶2). If Senate President is also unable: Speaker of the House acts. If both are unable: Congress shall enact a law determining who acts.

4. **Temporary disability (President transmits inability notice)** → VP acts as Acting President until President transmits recovery notice (Article VII, §11, ¶1)

5. **Contested disability** → VP and Cabinet majority transmit notice of inability; VP becomes Acting President; President may contest by transmitting recovery notice; Congress decides by two-thirds vote of all members in joint session if dispute is not resolved (Article VII, §11, ¶4)

**Critical**: "Acting President" is not "President." The Acting President during temporary disability does not exercise the full powers of the presidency under some interpretations — this is an unsettled question.

## Electoral Tribunal Jurisdiction

### Presidential Electoral Tribunal (PET)

**Composition**: The Supreme Court, sitting en banc, constitutes the PET. The Chief Justice presides.

**Jurisdiction**: Exclusive original jurisdiction over all contests relating to the election, returns, and qualifications of the President and Vice-President.

**Procedure**: Electoral protest filed within 30 days after proclamation. Recount, revision of ballots, appreciation of ballots. Three rounds of revision typically.

**Nature**: The PET is a separate tribunal, not the SC per se, though composed of SC justices. It has its own rules.

---

### Senate Electoral Tribunal (SET)

**Composition**: Three Supreme Court Justices designated by the Chief Justice, and six senators elected by each party on the basis of proportional representation. The senior Justice designated is the SET Chairman.

**Jurisdiction**: Sole judge of all contests relating to the election, returns, and qualifications of Senators. *Lazatin v. HRET* analog applies: jurisdiction is exclusive.

**Disqualification**: A senator whose election is being contested is disqualified from sitting in the SET for that contest.

---

### House of Representatives Electoral Tribunal (HRET)

**Composition**: Three SC Justices designated by CJ, plus six House members elected proportionally.

**Jurisdiction**: Sole judge of all contests relating to election, returns, and qualifications of House members. District and party-list members.

**Party-list**: HRET has jurisdiction over party-list seat contests. COMELEC retains jurisdiction over the party-list accreditation and allocation process.

---

## COMELEC Jurisdiction

**Original jurisdiction**: All contests involving elective regional, provincial, and city officials.

**Appellate jurisdiction**: Over all contests involving elective municipal and barangay officials.

**Administrative jurisdiction**: Registration of voters, candidates, political parties; enforcement of election laws; campaign finance; canvassing (for officials other than President/VP).

**Quasi-judicial functions**: Decides disqualification cases, cancellation of CoC, substitution of candidates.

**Limitations**: COMELEC cannot decide questions of citizenship or residence for senators, since those are within the exclusive jurisdiction of SET. Similarly, COMELEC's jurisdiction ends when jurisdiction of an electoral tribunal begins.

---

## Canvassing — Presidential and VP Elections

Congress, in joint session, canvasses the votes for President and VP. The Senate President presides. Congress may only open certificates of canvass and count votes — it has no authority to examine the qualifications of candidates at this stage. *Pimentel v. Joint Committee* (G.R. No. 163783, 2004): Congress acting as national board of canvassers has ministerial duties; it cannot go beyond the counting function.

---

## Failure of Elections

COMELEC has authority to declare failure of elections and call for special elections when: (a) election is not held on the date fixed; (b) election is suspended before the hour fixed by law for closing of voting; (c) after voting and during preparation and transmission of returns or in custody or canvassing, such election results in a failure to elect. All three grounds require the failure to affect the result of the election.

## Omnibus Election Code (B.P. 881) — Key Provisions

**Disqualification grounds**: Commission of any crime involving moral turpitude; acts constituting grounds for disqualification; giving money or other material consideration to influence votes; convictions within 5 years prior to election; perpetual disqualification from holding public office.

**Certificate of Candidacy cancellation**: Material misrepresentation of material qualification ground for CoC cancellation. *Mitra v. COMELEC* (G.R. No. 191938, 2010): cancellation requires deliberate misrepresentation.

**Substitution**: A candidate who withdraws, dies, or is disqualified may be substituted by a person belonging to the same party. No substitution for independent candidates.

---

## Automated Election System

*Roque v. COMELEC* (G.R. No. 188456, 2010): Automated election system under R.A. 9369 upheld as constitutional. COMELEC's administrative discretion in implementing AES upheld. Audit mechanisms required.

---

## Party-List System

**Constitutional basis**: Article VI, §5. Twenty percent of total House membership allocated to party-list representatives.

**Formula**: *Banat v. COMELEC* (G.R. No. 179271, 2009): two-round proportional allocation methodology; eliminated the two-percent threshold barrier for seat allocation (while maintaining it for participation) and implemented proportional allocation.

**Qualification**: National, regional, and sectoral parties representing marginalized and underrepresented sectors. *Atong Paglaum v. COMELEC* (G.R. No. 203766, 2013): substantially broadened the definition of qualified party-list groups; not limited to marginalized sectors; major political parties may participate through sectoral wings.

---

## Succession in Local Government

**Local Government Code, §44**: When a permanent vacancy exists in the Office of the Governor, Vice-Governor, Mayor, or Vice-Mayor, the highest-ranking Sanggunian member becomes the governor/vice-governor/mayor/vice-mayor. If permanent vacancy exists in the Sanggunian, the political party of the member who caused the vacancy shall appoint a replacement from a list of nominees.

**Appointive vs. elective local officials**: Different succession rules apply. Appointive positions in LGUs are filled by the appointing authority.
## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Election Law

Never state that the COMELEC has original jurisdiction over presidential and vice-presidential election contests — these belong exclusively to the Presidential Electoral Tribunal (PET), which is the Supreme Court sitting en banc. Never conflate the ministerial canvassing function of Congress (which cannot go beyond counting per *Pimentel*) with a qualifications review — Congress as national board of canvassers cannot revisit candidate qualifications at the canvassing stage. Never apply the *BANAT* party-list formula without flagging that its precise mechanics have been subject to ongoing dispute and COMELEC application problems — present the formula's general principle and flag uncertainty in application. Never state that COMELEC jurisdiction over electoral protests extends to senators — SET has exclusive jurisdiction over senator contests. Never invent specific election return figures, vote counts, or COMELEC resolution numbers — cite only general doctrinal principles unless a specific figure can be verified from official records.
