# Tax Administration

## Primary Sources
**NIRC §§1–14, 200–246** — BIR organization; Commissioner's powers; compliance; penalties. **BIR Revenue Regulations (RRs), Revenue Memorandum Orders (RMOs), Revenue Memorandum Circulars (RMCs)** — implement NIRC. BIR issuances are subordinate to the NIRC and cannot expand or restrict statutory powers.

---

## Bureau of Internal Revenue
**Commissioner of Internal Revenue (CIR)**: Appointed by the President; under the Department of Finance. Exercises quasi-judicial powers — decisions on protests, refunds, and rulings subject to CTA review.

**Powers (§4)**:
- Interpret NIRC provisions (subject to review by Secretary of Finance).
- Issue rulings and circulars.
- Assess and collect taxes; compromise and abate.
- Examine books, records, returns.
- Compel production of records (subpoena powers).

**BIR organizational units**: National Office; Revenue District Offices (RDOs) by geographic assignment; Large Taxpayer Service (LTS) for large taxpayer accounts.

---

## Large Taxpayer Service
**Classification criteria**: Asset size; gross sales/receipts; tax payments; specific industries (banking, oil, utilities). Large taxpayers assigned to Large Taxpayer District Offices (LTDOs).

**eFPS**: Electronic Filing and Payment System — **mandatory for large taxpayers**; optional for others. BIR RRs govern enrollment and mandatory use.

---

## Returns and Filing
**Annual ITR**: On or before **April 15** of the following taxable year (calendar year); 3 months after close of fiscal year (fiscal year taxpayers).

**Quarterly income tax**: Corporations file quarterly ITRs with advance payment; final adjustment on annual ITR.

**Amended returns**: Allowed before a final assessment is issued. Post-assessment amendments require CIR approval.

---

## Penalties
**Civil surcharges (§248)**:
- **25%** surcharge: late filing; late payment; wrong venue filing.
- **50%** surcharge: fraudulent return; willful neglect to file.

**Deficiency interest (§249)**: **12% per annum** (or double BSP legal rate, whichever is higher under TRAIN) on deficiency taxes from prescribed payment date until full payment.

**Delinquency interest**: Same rate on delinquent taxes from date of delinquency (assessment final and executory).

**Criminal penalties (§§254–275)**:
- **Failure to file** (§255): ₱10,000–₱25,000 fine + imprisonment.
- **Tax evasion (§254)**: ₱30,000–₱100,000 fine + 2–4 years imprisonment per count.
- **Falsification of returns**: criminal prosecution + civil penalties.
- **Failure to withhold**: agent personally liable + surcharges.

---

## Withholding Tax Administration
**Obligation**: Withholding agents must withhold on: compensation (monthly employers); expanded withholding tax (EWT) on professional fees, rentals, etc.; final withholding taxes; VAT withholding (government money payments).

**Remittance**: Monthly within prescribed deadlines. Large taxpayers: eFPS mandatory.

**BIR Form 2307 (EWT certificate)**: Issued to payee; credited against payee's income tax liability.

**BIR Form 2316 (compensation certificate)**: Issued to employees at year-end.

---

## Tax Clearance
Required for: importation; government contracts; real property transfers; release of insurance proceeds above threshold; court-supervised settlements. Obtained from BIR after settling all assessed and undisputed liabilities.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Tax Administration
BIR RRs, RMOs, and RMCs cannot expand or restrict the NIRC — they implement it; statutory tax rights and obligations derive from the NIRC. The 25%/50% surcharge attaches automatically — it is not discretionary. Deficiency interest under TRAIN accrues at 12% or double BSP legal rate (whichever higher) — NOT at the pre-TRAIN flat 20%; apply the correct rate for the relevant period. Annual ITR deadline is April 15 — extensions are possible by Secretary of Finance order in extraordinary circumstances; do not assume an extension without verified official announcement. Never advise taxpayers to rely on verbal BIR guidance — only written rulings have evidentiary weight. BIR Rulings are binding on the BIR and the taxpayer who sought the ruling; they do not constitute law or precedent for others.
**Assessment power (§6)**:
- Examine returns; make assessments when no return filed or return inaccurate.
- Determine gross income by any method that clearly reflects income if books inadequate.
- Access records of third parties (bank records, BSP data) after obtaining proper authorization.

**Compromise (§204[A])**: CIR may compromise: (1) doubtful validity of assessment; (2) financial incapacity. Minimum compromise rates: 40% of basic assessed tax (doubtful validity); 10% (financial incapacity). Board of Internal Revenue (BIR) approves compromises above ₱1M.

**Abatement (§204[B])**: CIR may abate or cancel tax liability if: (1) assessment is excessive and patently wrong; (2) administration/collection costs exceed amount to be collected.

**Prescriptive periods for assessment**: Normal — 3 years from last day to file return (or actual filing, whichever is later). False/fraudulent return or no return — 10 years from discovery. Waiver: taxpayer may waive prescription (BIR waiver must comply strictly with RMO 14-2016 formalities — prescribed form, signed by authorized CIR officer, notarized, within the prescriptive period; invalid waiver = assessment time-barred even if taxpayer signed).

## Assessment Process

**Letter of Authority (LOA)**: Prerequisite for valid audit; issued to specific BIR revenue officer; covers specific tax type and period. Audit conducted without LOA = invalid; assessment based on such audit void. *CIR v. Sony Philippines* (GR 178697): LOA is jurisdictional; re-assignment of audit to another officer requires new LOA or amendment.

**Preliminary Assessment Notice (PAN)**: Issued to taxpayer before formal assessment; gives taxpayer 15 days to respond; failure to respond does not automatically validate assessment — BIR must still consider response or explain why not.

**Final Assessment Notice (FAN) / Formal Letter of Demand (FLD)**: Issued with details of deficiency; starts 30-day protest period. Assessment becomes final if not protested within 30 days.

**Assessment notice requirements**: Must state law, facts, amount, period. Vague or general statements insufficient. *CIR v. Menguito* (GR 167560): assessment not supported by specific findings is void.

---

## Protest and Administrative Remedies

**Request for Reconsideration**: Protest on purely legal grounds; no new evidence submitted; BIR re-evaluates based on existing record. Filed within 30 days from FAN/FLD.

**Request for Reinvestigation**: Protest involving new evidence and documentary support; BIR must conduct new examination of additional documents. Filed within 30 days from FAN/FLD; additional 60 days to submit supporting documents after filing.

**BIR inaction**: If CIR fails to act on protest within 180 days (reinvestigation) — taxpayer may appeal to CTA within 30 days from lapse of 180 days OR wait for CIR decision.

**Final Decision on Disputed Assessment (FDDA)**: CIR's denial of protest or affirmation of assessment; must be in writing with basis stated. Taxpayer must appeal FDDA to CTA Division within 30 days of receipt. Failure to appeal FDDA within 30 days = assessment becomes final, executory, and demandable.

---

## Collection of Taxes

**Civil remedies for collection (§§205–221)**: (1) Tax lien — federal priority claim on all property of delinquent taxpayer; registered with ROD for real property; attachment effective upon filing; (2) Distraint — seizure of personal property; actual or constructive; (3) Levy — seizure and sale of real property; (4) Court action.

**Prescriptive period for collection**: 5 years from date assessment becomes final and demandable. In case of no assessment (e.g., self-assessed but unpaid): 5 years from date of filing or deadline. If assessment void: no collection — void assessment tolls nothing.

**Tax clearance**: Required for corporations applying for dissolution, businesses changing ownership, and certain government transactions. Issued only when all tax obligations settled.

---

## BIR Rulings and Interpretations

**Ruling request**: Taxpayer may seek BIR ruling on tax consequences of specific transaction; BIR opinion binding on BIR unless taxpayer misrepresented facts or law changed.

**RMC vs. RR**: Revenue Memorandum Circular — interprets existing law; not a regulation; cannot impose new obligations. Revenue Regulation — published in Official Gazette; requires proper promulgation; legislative in character if imposing new obligations. Courts review BIR issuances for consistency with NIRC — invalid if exceed statutory authority.

**Non-retroactivity of rulings (§246 NIRC)**: Revocation of BIR ruling not applied retroactively if taxpayer acted in good faith in reliance on the ruling. Exception: taxpayer misrepresented facts.

---

## Penalties and Surcharges

**Surcharge (§248)**: 25% for simple failure to file/pay on time; 50% for fraudulent return or willful neglect to file.

**Interest (§249)**: 12% per annum (post-CREATE) on unpaid tax from deadline to payment. Previously double the legal interest rate — CREATE fixed it at 12%.

**Compromise penalty**: In lieu of criminal prosecution; requires taxpayer consent; BIR issues schedule of compromise penalties per type of violation.

**Criminal liability (§§254–269)**: Willful failure to file, pay, keep records, supply information — fine and imprisonment. Corporate officers personally criminally liable if they participated in or directed the violation.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Tax Administration
Waiver of prescriptive period (LOA and waiver) must strictly comply with RMO 14-2016 formal requirements — courts invalidate waivers with technical defects even if taxpayer signed. Assessment without LOA is void — not merely voidable. 30-day protest deadline is prescriptive — no extension after FAN/FLD served. PAN non-response does not automatically validate FAN — BIR must still issue valid FAN. BIR rulings bind the BIR but can be challenged in court if inconsistent with NIRC. Criminal liability of officers is personal; corporate liability and officer liability are separate proceedings. Post-CREATE interest rate is fixed at 12% — never use old double-legal-rate formula.

---

## Run After Tax Evaders (RATE) Program

**RATE**: BIR program targeting high-profile tax evaders; cases referred to DOJ for preliminary investigation; criminal informations filed in RTC. Tax evasion charges under §§254–269 NIRC.

**Elements of tax evasion (§254)**: Willful failure to pay tax; willful failure to file return; willful making of false/fraudulent return. Willfulness is essential — distinguished from civil negligence. Proof of tax due + failure to pay + willfulness = conviction.

**Conspiracy**: Corporate officers who participate in the decision not to file or pay are criminally liable jointly with the corporation; acquittal of corporation does not preclude officer's conviction.

**Tax fraud vs. tax avoidance**: Tax avoidance = legal reduction of tax using lawful means; not punishable. Tax evasion = fraudulent/illegal means to avoid tax; criminal. Courts look to substance over form — transactions structured solely for tax evasion treated as evasion even if form is legal. *CIR v. The Estates of Benigno Toda Jr.* (GR 147188): estate sale transaction recharacterized as tax evasion scheme.

## Transfer Pricing

**Section 50 NIRC (arm's length standard)**: CIR may distribute, apportion, or allocate gross income, deductions, credits, or allowances between related taxpayers when necessary to clearly reflect income or prevent tax evasion. Covers controlled transactions between related parties — same as OECD arm's length standard.

**BIR RR 2-2013 (Transfer Pricing Regulations)**: Implements §50; adopts OECD Guidelines; mandates use of most appropriate method (CUP, RPM, CPM, PSM, TNMM); contemporaneous documentation required for covered taxpayers; BIR may adjust transfer prices to arm's length equivalents.

**Documentation**: Transfer pricing documentation must be prepared before filing income tax return for the period; covers: description of controlled transactions; selected method and reason; benchmarking analysis; financial data. Failure to maintain contemporaneous documentation: prima facie evidence that the taxpayer's transfer prices are not at arm's length.

---

## BIR Electronic Systems

**eFPS (Electronic Filing and Payment System)**: Mandatory for large taxpayers, top corporations, government agencies. Electronic filing via eFPS; payment through authorized agent banks online. Late filing via eFPS when mandatory: penalty applies (₱1,000 for late filing plus 25% surcharge on tax due).

**BIR Online Registration and Update System (ORUS)**: New system replacing manual TIN registration; online BIR Form 1901/1902/1903/1904 filing; simplifies TIN acquisition and taxpayer record updates.

**Computerized Accounting Systems (CAS)**: Large taxpayers required to maintain CAS; BIR-approved; data submitted in prescribed format on audit. Subsystem components: sales; purchases; payroll; general ledger.

---

## Tax Clearance and Certificates

**Tax clearance (BIR Form 0937)**: Issued by BIR certifying no outstanding liability; required for: government contracts; dissolution of corporation; certain BSP transactions. Processing: taxpayer applies; BIR checks against outstanding assessments; clearance valid for period specified.

**Certificate of Tax Exemption**: Issued to non-profit organizations, cooperatives, and other entities claiming income tax exemption under specific NIRC provisions or special laws; must be renewed periodically; exempt status not self-executing — formal certification required.

**Electronic Certificate Authorizing Registration (eCAR)**: Required for transfer of real property, shares, or other registrable property; issued after verification that CGT, DST, estate tax, or donor's tax has been paid. Registry offices (ROD, LTO) cannot process transfer without eCAR.

---

## Key Cases — Tax Administration
**CIR v. Enron Subic Power Corp.** (GR 166387): BIR must furnish taxpayer with the bases for assessment; assessment letter that merely states amount of deficiency without specifying the factual and legal bases is void for denial of due process.

**CIR v. Sony Philippines** (GR 178697): Letter of Authority (LOA) is a jurisdictional prerequisite; BIR examiner who audits without LOA or beyond its scope has no authority; resulting assessment is void regardless of the amount of tax actually due.

**Fitness by Design v. CIR** (GR 215957): FAN must be issued within 15 days from expiration of the period given to the taxpayer to respond to the PAN — missing this deadline violates due process and invalidates the FAN.

**CIR v. Transitions Optical Philippines** (GR 227928): Waiver of prescriptive period must strictly comply with BIR-prescribed formalities (RMO 20-90 superseded by RMO 14-2016); failure to have waiver signed by proper BIR official within valid period = waiver void = assessment time-barred even if taxpayer signed.

## Taxpayer Registration and TIN
**Tax Identification Number (TIN)**: Every person subject to any tax under NIRC must register with BIR and secure TIN. Individuals — one TIN per person for life; cannot have multiple TINs (criminal offense). Corporations — TIN issued at registration; branch offices use same TIN with branch code suffix.
**Registration requirements**: BIR Form 1901 (self-employed/professionals); 1902 (employees); 1903 (corporations/partnerships); 1904 (one-time transactions/estates/trusts). Registration must occur before start of business operations.
**Books of accounts**: All persons required to file income tax returns must maintain books of accounts (journal, ledger, subsidiary books). Large taxpayers: computerized accounting system (CAS) required; BIR permits must be secured before use.
**Annual information return (§§ 255 et seq.)**: Employers file annual alphabetical list of employees (BIR Form 1604-C) showing compensation and taxes withheld; withholding agents file 1604-E and 1604-F for expanded and final withholding taxes; supporting BIR Form 2307/2316 issued to payees.
**Cessation of business**: File notice of closure with BIR; surrender COR (Certificate of Registration); pay all outstanding taxes; BIR issues Tax Clearance; submit inventory of unused official receipts/invoices for cancellation.
