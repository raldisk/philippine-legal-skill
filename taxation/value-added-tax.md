# Value-Added Tax

## Primary Sources
**NIRC Title IV (§§105–115) as amended by TRAIN (R.A. 10963) and CREATE (R.A. 11534)** — VAT. BIR Revenue Regulations implement VAT rules; BIR issuances govern compliance.

---

## Nature
VAT is an indirect tax on value added at each stage of production and distribution. Economic burden passes to final consumer. Formula: **Output Tax − Input Tax = VAT Payable**. Standard rate: **12%** on gross selling price or gross value of goods sold/leased, or gross receipts from services.

---

## Registration
**Mandatory (§236[G])**: Gross sales/receipts exceed **₱3 million** in any 12-month period → mandatory VAT registration under TRAIN.

**Optional**: Below threshold may opt in.

**OPT (§116)**: Non-VAT registered persons subject to Other Percentage Tax — 3% (1% under CREATE for July 1, 2020–June 30, 2023; restored to 3%).

---

## VAT-able Transactions
**Sale of goods/properties (§106)**: Transfer of ownership or title. Gross selling price = actual price; if below cost without credible reason, FMV applies.

**Sale of services (§108)**: All services for fee — construction, lessors, restaurants, professionals, and others.

**Importation (§107)**: VAT at time goods enter Philippines. Import VAT paid is creditable input tax if goods used in VAT-able business.

---

## Zero-Rated vs. Exempt — Critical Distinction
| | Zero-Rated | VAT-Exempt |
|---|---|---|
| Output tax | 0% | None |
| Input tax credits | **Yes — creditable/refundable** | **No — not creditable** |
| Examples | Export sales; services to non-residents paid in foreign currency | Agricultural food in original state; educational services; residential lots ≤ ₱1.5M; residential condo ≤ ₱2.5M; medical/hospital services; bank services |

---

## Input and Output Tax
**Output tax**: 12% × gross selling price/gross receipts.

**Input tax sources**: Purchase from VAT-registered suppliers; importations; transitional input tax; presumptive input tax (for food processors); input on depreciable capital goods (spread over 60 months if > ₱1M).

**Input tax credit**: Deduct from output tax. Excess → carry over to next period OR (for zero-rated only) claim as cash refund or tax credit certificate (TCC).

**Input tax refund (§112)**: Taxpayers may file for refund/TCC of input taxes attributable to zero-rated sales. **Prescriptive period: 2 years from close of the taxable quarter when the zero-rated sales were made.** CTA has jurisdiction over denied or unacted refund claims.

---

## Compliance
**Filing**: Quarterly VAT return (Form 2550Q) under TRAIN — monthly declaration abolished for regular VAT taxpayers. Large taxpayers: follow eFPS requirements.

**Invoice requirements**: VAT-registered sellers must issue VAT invoices (goods) or VAT official receipts (services) showing VAT separately. Failure → input tax claim by buyer may be disallowed.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — VAT
Never confuse zero-rated and VAT-exempt — zero-rating allows input tax recovery; exemption does not. The ₱3M threshold is under TRAIN; verify against any subsequent amendment. The 2-year prescriptive period for VAT refunds runs from the close of the taxable quarter when zero-rated sales were made — not from the date of filing or date of payment. Under TRAIN, monthly VAT declarations were abolished for regular VAT taxpayers — but verify whether a taxpayer is classified as a large taxpayer with different filing obligations. Input tax on capital goods > ₱1M must be spread over 60 months or its useful life — not claimed in full in the month of purchase. **NIRC §§105–115 as amended by TRAIN (R.A. 10963) and CREATE (R.A. 11534)** — VAT imposition, rates, exemptions. **BIR Revenue Regulations No. 13-2018 (TRAIN VAT IRR)**, **RR 15-2022 (CREATE VAT amendments)**. **Invoicing requirements** under separate BIR RRs.

---

## Nature of VAT
**Indirect tax**: Imposed on seller/service provider; economic burden passed on to buyer. Seller liable to BIR regardless of collection from buyer. *CIR v. CA and Commonwealth Management* (GR 124043): VAT is an indirect tax on consumption; the statutory taxpayer (seller) is separate from the economic bearer (buyer).

**Invoice method**: VAT computed on each sale; input tax from purchases credited against output tax on sales; only net VAT remitted to BIR. Creates self-policing mechanism — each buyer wants invoice to claim input credit.

---

## Coverage — Transactions Subject to VAT
**12% VAT on (§106)**:
- Sale, barter, or exchange of goods or properties in the course of trade or business.
- Importation of goods into the Philippines — VAT imposed on the importer regardless of business purpose.
- Sale of services and use/lease of properties (§108).

**Threshold**: VAT applies only to persons with gross sales/receipts exceeding ₱3,000,000 per year (TRAIN). Persons below threshold: exempt from VAT; may be subject to 3% percentage tax (§116); or elect 8% flat income tax (if individual).

**Gross selling price**: For goods — total amount received excluding VAT. For services — gross receipts actually or constructively received.

---

## VAT-Exempt Transactions (§109)

| Category | Specific Transactions |
|---|---|
| Agricultural | Sale of raw agricultural products; marine/forest products in original state; livestock/poultry for breeding |
| Basic necessities | Sale of fertilizers, seeds, seedlings, fingerlings, fish/livestock/poultry feeds, ingredients for such feeds |
| Health and education | Services rendered by educational institutions; medical/dental/hospital/veterinary services |
| Financial services | Financial intermediary services (banks); lending activities of financing companies |
| Housing | Sale of residential property with selling price ≤ ₱3.2M (residential dwelling) |
| Government | Import of personal and household effects of OFWs returning permanently |
| Cultural | Sales by cooperatives with accumulated reserves below ₱10M |
| Fuel/power | Sale of power generated from renewable energy sources |
| Transport | International air/sea transport of passengers |

**Note**: Threshold amounts for housing VAT exemptions are subject to adjustment by BIR (verify current thresholds under applicable RR).

## Zero-Rated Sales (§§106[A][2], 108[B])

**Export sales**: Sales of goods to foreign buyer; payment in foreign currency; goods physically shipped out of the Philippines. Zero-rated means VAT rate is 0% but transaction is still a VAT transaction — seller may claim input tax refund.

**Other zero-rated sales**:
- Foreign currency denominated sales (goods destined for export economy).
- Sales to registered export enterprises by their local suppliers (CREATE amendment; subject to BIR registration and compliance).
- Sales to persons entitled to VAT zero-rating under special laws (PEZA-registered enterprises in ecozones).

**Services zero-rated (§108[B])**:
- Services rendered to non-residents — consideration paid in foreign currency and remitted through BSP.
- Services to PEZA-registered enterprises performing export-oriented activities.
- BPO/IT-BPM services rendered to non-residents — must meet foreign currency remittance and non-resident requirement.

---

## Input Tax Credits

**Eligible input taxes**:
- VAT paid on purchase of goods and services used in VAT-registered activities.
- VAT paid on importation of goods.
- VAT on leases and rentals.
- Transitional input tax: 2% of value of beginning inventory upon VAT registration.

**Creditable input tax timing**: Generally claimable in the period of payment. Large taxpayers: BIR may require monthly allocation.

**Input tax on capital goods**: Spread over useful life (or 60 months, whichever is shorter) if costing more than ₱1 million. Monthly amortization reduces the distortion of large capital purchases.

**Non-creditable input tax**: VAT on purchases not related to VAT-registered activities; input tax on purchases from non-VAT-registered suppliers; input tax on exempt transactions.

---

## VAT Refund — Excess Input Tax

**Refund for zero-rated sellers**: Zero-rated sellers accrue input tax from purchases but have no output tax to offset against. Net input tax becomes refundable or creditable.

**Filing timeline (§112[A])**: Administrative claim for refund/TCC with CIR — within 2 years from close of taxable quarter when sales were made.

**BIR processing (§112[C])**: BIR must decide within 90 days from submission of complete documents. If denied or no action: file judicial claim with CTA within 30 days from denial or lapse of 90 days.

**Sequential period rule**: Following *San Roque* — 90-day administrative period must be allowed to lapse before CTA filing is ripe. Premature CTA petition dismissed for lack of jurisdiction. (Note: 90-day period under §112 vs. 120-day period under other provisions — verify specific provision).

---

## Invoicing Requirements

**VAT invoice/official receipt**: Mandatory for every VAT-registered person making a sale. Must show: name, address, TIN of seller and buyer; date; description of goods/services; quantity; unit price; amount excluding VAT; VAT amount; total amount with VAT.

**Consequences of non-issuance**: Seller: administrative penalty; buyer: cannot claim input tax credit. Invoice requirements strictly enforced — minor defects may not invalidate unless material.

**Electronic invoicing (EIS)**: BIR implementing mandatory electronic invoicing for large taxpayers in phases; system-generated e-invoices accepted; must comply with BIR data format requirements.

---

## Transitional and Special Issues

**PEZA vs. non-PEZA suppliers**: Effective zero-rating for sales to PEZA enterprises inside ecozones treats the ecozones as foreign territory for VAT purposes (*Toshiba Information Equipment v. CIR*, GR 157594). Local suppliers to PEZA must comply with VAT zero-rating registration to claim input VAT refund.

**Cross-border doctrine**: VAT zero-rating for exports is based on the destination principle — tax where goods/services are consumed; Philippines does not tax what is consumed abroad.

**Digital services (R.A. 11976, 2024)**: Non-resident digital service providers (Netflix, Google, etc.) required to register for VAT with BIR and collect 12% VAT on digital services sold to Philippine consumers. Verify BIR implementing rules for current compliance requirements.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — VAT
VAT threshold is ₱3,000,000 (TRAIN) — never use pre-TRAIN ₱1,919,500 threshold. Zero-rated ≠ exempt: zero-rated sellers may claim input tax refund; exempt sellers cannot. Input tax on capital goods >₱1M must be amortized over useful life or 60 months — not immediately creditable in full. VAT refund for zero-rated sellers: 90-day administrative wait before CTA filing (§112) — premature filing dismissed. Housing VAT exemption thresholds are periodically adjusted by BIR — never cite a specific peso amount without verification. Digital services VAT (R.A. 11976) applies to non-resident providers — relatively new; check current BIR implementing rules. Cross-border doctrine means services must be shown to be consumed outside Philippines to qualify for zero-rating — mere contractual provision to foreign entity insufficient if service is rendered locally.

---

## Percentage Tax — Non-VAT Entities

**Section 116 percentage tax**: 3% on gross quarterly sales/receipts of persons not VAT-registered and not subject to other percentage taxes; CREATE temporarily reduced to 1% (July 1, 2020 to June 30, 2023); restored to 3% after June 30, 2023.

**Who pays percentage tax**: Persons with gross sales/receipts below ₱3M VAT threshold; persons specifically subject to percentage tax regardless of threshold (e.g., common carriers, radio/TV, banks, life insurance).

**Persons specifically subject to percentage tax (§§117–128)**:
- Domestic carriers and keepers of garages — 3% on quarterly gross receipts.
- International air/sea carriers — 3% on gross receipts from cargo/mail originating from Philippines.
- Franchise grantees — 3% (radio/TV broadcasting with gross receipts ≤ ₱10M); or 2% (gas/water utilities).
- Banks and quasi-banks — 5% on gross receipts (financial intermediation).
- Life insurance companies — 2% on premiums.
- Agents of foreign insurance — 4%.
- Cockpits — 18% on gross receipts.
- Cabarets, night/day clubs — 18%.
- Jai-alai/horse races — 10% on gross receipts of operator.
- Winnings — 10% on net winnings.

## VAT Compliance and Audit Issues

**Output tax allocation**: If taxpayer has both VAT-taxable and VAT-exempt sales, input taxes must be allocated — pro-rata or actual tracing method. Input taxes attributable to exempt sales are NOT creditable; added to cost of exempt goods/services.

**VAT on real property**:
- Sale of residential lots: VAT applies if consideration exceeds ₱1.9 million (residential lot) or ₱3.2 million (residential dwellings) — verify current BIR thresholds under applicable RR; TRAIN adjusted these.
- Sale of commercial property: subject to VAT if seller is VAT-registered or required to register.
- Lease of residential unit: exempt if monthly rent ≤ ₱15,000 per unit; commercial leases — VAT applies.

**VAT on importation**: 12% VAT on goods imported into the Philippines; assessed on: (1) dutiable value (customs value) + customs duties + excise taxes + other charges; collected by BOC before release from customs custody. Importer may claim as input tax if imported goods are used in VAT-registered business.

**VAT withholding**: Government withholding agents (national agencies, GOCCs, LGUs) required to withhold 5% or 10% of VAT component on purchases from VAT-registered suppliers; remit directly to BIR; issue Certificate of Creditable VAT Withheld (BIR Form 2307). Supplier's input tax offset: withheld VAT is treated as creditable input tax of the government.

---

## Common VAT Disputes

**Sale vs. assignment of receivables**: Assignment/sale of receivables arising from VAT-able transactions — not a separate VAT transaction if original transaction already subjected to VAT. However, sale of receivables for a discount may be a VAT-able financial service if the assignee is a financing entity.

**VAT on contributions to cooperatives**: Contributions by members to cooperative — exempt from VAT; but goods sold by cooperative to non-members — subject to VAT.

**Joint ventures**: Joint venture for construction registered with PCAB — exempt from VAT on its VAT-exempt construction activities. Joint venture NOT registered or for other purpose — subject to VAT on its transactions.

**Transitional provisions for VAT registration**: Person crossing the ₱3M threshold mid-year must register as VAT taxpayer within 10 days from end of month when threshold is exceeded; liable for VAT from that date; transitional input tax of 2% on beginning VAT-able inventory.

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — VAT Update
Percentage tax rate was temporarily reduced to 1% under CREATE (July 2020 – June 2023); now restored to 3% — verify period for disputes involving those years. VAT on residential property has peso threshold limits — always verify current BIR-published threshold (subject to inflation adjustment). Excess input tax on zero-rated sales is refundable — BUT the 90-day administrative claim period is mandatory before CTA. Joint venture for PCAB-registered construction — VAT exempt on those specific activities; non-registered JVs are not exempt. Government withholding VAT agents remit 5%/10% (depends on nature of purchase) directly to BIR; supplier nets the remaining VAT out. Never state that all lease transactions are VAT-exempt — monthly residential rent threshold applies; commercial leases are VAT-able.
