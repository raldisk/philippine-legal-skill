# Tax Remedies

## Primary Sources
**NIRC §§203–228** — assessment and collection. **§229** — refund claims. **R.A. 1125 as amended by R.A. 9282 and R.A. 9503** — Court of Tax Appeals (CTA). CTA has exclusive appellate jurisdiction over tax matters.

---

## BIR Assessment Process
**Pre-Assessment Notice (PAN)**: Required before Final Assessment Notice (FAN) in most cases. Taxpayer has **15 days** to respond.

**Final Assessment Notice (FAN) / Formal Letter of Demand**: Must state facts, law, rules, and regulations. Vague/conclusory FAN may be void. *CIR v. Enron Subic Power Corp.*: FAN must contain a definite demand.

**Prescriptive periods for assessment (§203, §222)**:
- **3-year period** (regular): from date return filed, or from last day for filing — whichever is **later**.
- **10-year period (§222[a])**: applies to **false/fraudulent returns** with intent to evade, or **non-filing**. Period runs from discovery of the fraud or non-filing.
- **Waiver**: Taxpayer and CIR may extend by written waiver before expiration. Must comply with RMO requirements. Defective waivers do not toll prescription. *CIR v. Systems Technology Institute*: non-compliant waiver = no extension.

---

## Taxpayer Protest
**Types of protest (§228)**:
- **Request for Reconsideration**: disputes the law/rules; no new evidence.
- **Request for Reinvestigation**: disputes facts; submits additional documents.

**Deadlines**:
- Protest: **30 days** from receipt of FAN.
- Submission of documents (reinvestigation): **60 days** from filing of protest.

**CIR inaction**: If CIR fails to act within **180 days** from submission of documents → taxpayer may appeal to CTA within **30 days** from lapse of the 180-day period.

**CIR decision**: If CIR denies (wholly or partly) → appeal to CTA Division within **30 days** from receipt of decision.

---

## Tax Refund and Credit
**Grounds (§229)**: Tax collected erroneously/illegally; tax paid without authority; penalty not subject to forfeiture; tax wrongly withheld.

**Prescriptive period: 2 years from date of payment** — strict jurisdictional requirement. Both administrative claim (with BIR) and judicial petition (with CTA) must be filed within 2 years.

**Procedure**: (1) Written claim with BIR. (2) If BIR denies or fails to act, file petition for review with CTA. *CIR v. Victorias Milling*: the 2-year period applies to both the administrative claim and the CTA petition.

---

## Court of Tax Appeals
**Structure**: CTA Division (3 judges) → CTA En Banc → Supreme Court (by certiorari).

**Exclusive jurisdiction**: Appeals from CIR decisions; BOC decisions; RTC tax cases; criminal tax cases.

**Payment not required for appeal**: A disputed assessment may be appealed to CTA without prior payment of the assessed tax (subject to injunction or bond in appropriate cases).

---

## BIR Collection Remedies
**Administrative (after assessment final and demandable)**: Distraint of personal property; levy on real property.

**Judicial**: Civil action for collection; criminal prosecution for tax evasion.

**Prescriptive period for collection (§222[c])**: 5 years from assessment becoming final and executory; 10 years for fraudulent/non-filed returns.

---

## Compromise and Abatement (§204)
**Compromise**: CIR may compromise when (1) assessment is of doubtful validity or (2) taxpayer is financially incapable.

**Abatement**: CIR may abate or cancel when assessment is unjust/excessive or collection costs exceed tax due.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Tax Remedies
The 3-year assessment period runs from the date of filing or the last day for filing — whichever is LATER, not from the end of the taxable year. The 10-year period applies only to false/fraudulent returns or non-filing — not to simple under-reporting without intent to evade. Defective waivers do not extend prescription — strict compliance with RMO required. The 180-day inaction period runs from submission of documents (for reinvestigation), not from filing of the protest. The 2-year refund period applies to both the BIR claim and the CTA petition — filing only with BIR does not toll the CTA period. Never state that payment of the assessed tax is a prerequisite to CTA jurisdiction over a disputed assessment.## Primary Sources

## Overview of Tax Dispute Levels
Tax disputes proceed: BIR Assessment → Administrative Protest → CIR Decision/Inaction → CTA Division (appeal) → CTA En Banc (appeal) → Supreme Court (Rule 45 petition). Knowing where the case sits determines applicable deadlines and remedies.

---

## Administrative Remedies

**For assessment disputes**:
1. File protest (Request for Reconsideration or Reinvestigation) within 30 days from FAN/FLD.
2. Submit additional evidence within 60 days from protest (reinvestigation only).
3. Wait for CIR decision; or after 180 days of inaction (reinvestigation), elect to appeal to CTA.

**For refund/credit**:
1. Administrative claim with CIR: within 2 years from date of payment.
2. If CIR denies or fails to act within 120 days: file judicial claim with CTA within 30 days from denial or lapse of 120 days.
3. Total period (administrative + judicial): must be initiated within 2-year prescriptive period from payment. *CIR v. Aichi Forging* (GR 184823): 120-day + 30-day periods are mandatory and jurisdictional.

---

## CTA Jurisdiction

**CTA Division (§7, R.A. 9282)**:
- Decisions of CIR on disputed assessments, refund claims, penalties.
- Decisions of Commissioner of Customs on: assessments; seizure/forfeiture; legal liability; customs protests.
- Decisions of Regional Trial Courts in local tax cases (on appeal).
- Criminal offenses arising from violations of NIRC and customs laws.
- Tax collection cases where principal amount is ≥ ₱1 million.

**CTA En Banc**: Reviews decisions of CTA Divisions by verified petition for review; final on questions of law with limited SC review.

**Supreme Court**: Petition for review on certiorari under Rule 45 — only pure questions of law; CTA factual findings conclusive if supported by substantial evidence.

---

## Judicial Remedies — Assessment Disputes

**Appeal from FAN (after administrative exhaustion)**: Appeal FDDA to CTA Division within 30 days of receipt.

**Appeal from BIR inaction**: If CIR fails to decide protest within 180 days — taxpayer may file with CTA within 30 days from lapse of 180-day period.

**Injunction in tax cases**: Generally not available (NIRC §218); exception only for irreparable injury and when collections are patently illegal. Courts deny injunctions liberally in tax cases.

**No injunction rule (§218)**: No court shall issue injunction to restrain collection of any national internal revenue tax, fee, or charge. CTA may suspend collection if it determines that collection would jeopardize taxpayer interest AND taxpayer deposits cash bond or surety bond equivalent to assessment.

---

## Refund and Tax Credit Certificate (TCC)

**Who may claim**: Taxpayer who has erroneously/excessively paid tax; or who has overpaid withholding tax; or entitled to tax credit for excess CWT.

**Modes of recovery**: (1) Refund in cash; (2) Tax Credit Certificate (TCC) — used to offset future BIR obligations; TCCs may be sold or assigned (subject to BIR/DOF regulations on TCC monetization).

**TCC validity**: Valid for 5 years from date of issuance; non-transferable unless expressly authorized; fraudulent TCC is void.

**Excess input VAT refund (§112)**: Filed with CIR; 90-day processing period; if denied or unanswered → appeal to CTA within 30 days. *CIR v. San Roque* (GR 187485) ruling: 120-day administrative period is mandatory; filing before lapse makes CTA filing premature and dismissible for lack of jurisdiction. *Transitional rules*: consult BIR MC issued after San Roque for transition period relief.

---

## Compromise and Abatement

**Compromise (§204[A])**:
- Doubtful validity: minimum 40% of basic assessment (20% if with financial incapacity + doubtful validity per BIR evaluation).
- Financial incapacity: minimum 10% of basic assessment; taxpayer must prove net worth is less than total assessed tax liability.
- Not available for: withheld taxes (employer's liability is a trust; not purely a tax debt of the withholding agent); criminal cases involving fraud; cases already decided by CTA or courts.

**Abatement (§204[B])**: All penalties/surcharges/interest cancelled if assessment is excessive, patently wrong, or collection costs exceed tax. Abatement covers only penalties — not the basic tax.

---

## Collection Remedies

**Distraint (§§207–208)**: Seizure of personal property; notice to taxpayer and third-party possessors of taxpayer's property; actual distraint (physical seizure); constructive distraint (taxpayer executes receipt as custodian). Third party holding property who ignores distraint notice: personally liable for value of property.

**Levy (§§207[B], 213)**: Seizure of real property; registered with Register of Deeds; forecloses after advertisement and auction; redemption period for delinquent taxpayer (usually 1 year); proceeds applied to tax, surcharges, interest, costs.

**Warrant of distraint/levy**: Issued by CIR or authorized officers; can be issued simultaneously with assessment if taxpayer is known to be trying to remove property.

---

## Taxpayer's Charter and Rights

**Taxpayer Bill of Rights (EO 44 and RMO 5-2000)**: Taxpayer has right to: courteous treatment; clear explanation of assessment; prompt receipt of refunds; examination only once for same year and same tax type (unless fraud involved or unusual circumstances); be informed of rights at start of audit.

**Right against double audit**: Taxpayer audited for a year may not be audited again for the same year and same tax, unless: fraud; new or additional information; request by taxpayer; joint audit with foreign tax authority.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Tax Remedies
CTA jurisdiction is statutory and exclusive — courts without CTA delegation have no jurisdiction over tax cases within CTA scope. 30-day appeal deadline from FDDA or BIR inaction is jurisdictional — no extension, no exception. Administrative claim for refund must be filed first before judicial claim. 120-day period for VAT refund is mandatory (San Roque); premature judicial filing = dismissed. Injunction against tax collection is the exception, not the rule — §218 applies unless patently illegal and irreparable injury shown. Compromise is not available for withholding tax liabilities (these are trust funds). Abatement covers penalties only — the basic tax must still be paid. TCC validity is 5 years — verify before attempting to use for credit offset.

## Prescriptive Periods — Consolidated Summary

| Remedy | Period | Counted From |
|---|---|---|
| Assessment (normal) | 3 years | Later of: return due date or actual filing date |
| Assessment (fraud/no return) | 10 years | Date of discovery of fraud or date return was due |
| Collection (from final assessment) | 5 years | Date assessment became final |
| Protest filing | 30 days | Receipt of FAN/FLD |
| Appeal to CTA (from FDDA) | 30 days | Receipt of FDDA |
| Appeal to CTA (BIR inaction) | 30 days | After 180-day inaction period |
| Refund claim — administrative | 2 years | Date of payment |
| Refund claim — judicial | 30 days | After 90/120-day administrative period |

**Tolling of prescription**: Suspension of assessment period: (a) CIR is prohibited from making assessment by court order; (b) taxpayer requests reinvestigation — period suspended from filing of request to 60 days after decision (BIR must show reinvestigation was actively pursued); (c) taxpayer cannot be located. Waiver of prescription: must comply with strict formal requirements (RMO 14-2016).

## Remedies for Unjust Assessment

**Letter of Authority defects**: Assessment based on audit conducted without or beyond LOA scope is void. Taxpayer must raise LOA defect during protest — failure to raise may be deemed waiver in some cases. RMO 43-90: LOA validity is 30 days from issuance; BIR must complete audit within the LOA period or revalidate.

**Violation of due process in assessment**: PAN must be issued before FAN; FAN must state facts and law; failure to do so voids the assessment. *CIR v. Fitness by Design* (GR 215957): FAN must be issued within 15 days from receipt of taxpayer's response to PAN; otherwise assessment void.

**Estoppel against BIR**: BIR may be estopped from collecting if: it issued a ruling in taxpayer's favor; taxpayer relied on that ruling; BIR subsequently changed position without retroactive effect. *CIR v. CA and Atlas Consolidated Mining* (GR 104151): BIR bound by its own rulings; revocation cannot be retroactive if taxpayer acted in good faith.

## CTA — Practice and Procedure

**Composition**: CTA en banc (Presiding Justice + 8 Associate Justices); CTA in Division (3 justices per division, 2 required for quorum). Appeals from Divisions: to En Banc. CTA decisions: 30 days from filing of petition, or extended upon proper motion.

**Contents of petition for review (CTA)**: Verified; attach: FDDA or order being reviewed; proof of timely filing; certification of non-forum shopping; all documentary evidence relied upon.

**Evidence in CTA**: Parties file judicial affidavits with exhibits; cross-examination at hearing; CTA may refer technical questions to expert. CTA findings of fact are conclusive if supported by substantial evidence — SC review on questions of law only.

**New issues on appeal**: Generally, issues not raised at administrative level cannot be raised for first time on CTA appeal. Exception: jurisdictional matters; issues apparent on face of record.

---

## Local Tax Remedies — CTA Jurisdiction Expansion

**R.A. 9282 §7(a)(3)**: CTA has exclusive original jurisdiction over tax collection cases involving amounts of at least ₱1 million (national taxes). For local taxes: RTC has original jurisdiction; CTA has appellate jurisdiction from RTC local tax decisions.

**Local tax refund procedure**: (1) Pay tax; (2) File protest with local treasurer — within 60 days of assessment or within 2 years of payment for overpayment; (3) Local treasurer decides; (4) Appeal to RTC; (5) RTC decision appealed to CTA (under R.A. 9282 §7[a][3]) as amended.

**Customs cases**: CTA has exclusive original jurisdiction on: seizure and forfeiture proceedings (decisions of Collector of Customs and Commissioner of Customs); protests on customs duties and fees; decisions of the Secretary of Finance on customs matters (appeal from BOC Commissioner).
