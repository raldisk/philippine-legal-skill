# Local Taxation

## Primary Sources
**Local Government Code (R.A. 7160), Book II Title I (§§129–212)** — local taxes, fees, charges. **Book II §§232–255** — real property taxation. ***Mandanas v. Ochoa* (G.R. No. 199802, July 3, 2018; finality April 2021)** — IRA base expanded to all national taxes (now called National Tax Allotment, NTA).

---

## Local Business Tax (LBT, §§143–152)
**Power**: Municipalities and cities may impose LBT on: manufacturers/assemblers/repackers (on gross sales); wholesalers/distributors/dealers (on gross sales); retailers; contractors/service enterprises (on gross receipts); banks and financial institutions (on gross receipts); exporters.

**Rate limits**: LGC sets maximum rates. Cities may levy up to 50% more than the rates set for municipalities.

**Situs of taxation**: LBT paid where the principal office or branch is located. Allocation rules apply when sales are made in multiple LGUs.

**Frequency**: Quarterly; annual registration fee required.

---

## Real Property Tax (RPT, §§232–255)
**Authority**: Provinces, cities, municipalities within Metro Manila levy annual ad valorem RPT.

**Maximum rates (§233)**: Provinces — 1% of assessed value. Cities and MM municipalities — 2% of assessed value.

**Assessment process**:
- Local assessor determines market value per Schedule of Market Values (SMV).
- Assessment level (set by ordinance per property classification) applied to market value.
- **Assessed value = Market value × Assessment level.**
- Property classifications: residential, commercial, industrial, agricultural — each has different assessment levels.

**Special Education Fund (SEF) levy (§235)**: Additional 1% on assessed value; accrues to local school board.

**Idle land tax (§237)**: Up to 5% on assessed value of idle agricultural land not cultivated for ≥ 1 year; idle urban land > 1,000 sqm generating no income.

**RPT exemptions (§234)**: Real property of the Republic/political subdivisions; charitable institutions, churches, parsonages, mosques, non-profit cemeteries actually, directly, exclusively used for religious/charitable/educational purposes; machineries for pollution control; cooperatives under R.A. 6938. Exemption requires actual, direct, exclusive use — partial use does not qualify.

**Delinquency**: Interest at 2% per month; maximum 36 months. LGU may foreclose after notice + public auction.

## Other Local Taxes
**Community tax (cedula)**: Individuals ₱5 + additions; corporations ₱500 + additions based on income and property. Required for legal transactions.

**Transfer tax on real property**: Province/city may impose up to 50% of 1% of consideration or FMV (whichever higher) on every sale/donation/barter/other transfer of real property.

**Professional tax (§139)**: Cities/municipalities may impose on professionals practicing within the LGU.

**Amusement tax (§140)**: On admission fees to theaters, cinemas, concert halls.

---

## Prohibited Local Taxes (§133)
LGUs CANNOT impose: income taxes (except on banks); estate, inheritance, gift taxes; customs duties; documentary stamp taxes; taxes on premiums paid on insurance; taxes on freight/cargo vessel receipts; taxes on national government agencies.

---

## LGU Revenue Sharing — NTA
Post-*Mandanas*: NTA = LGU share of all national taxes collected, not just BIR collections. DILG and DBM directives govern phased implementation. LGU shares: 40% (provinces); 45% (cities); 35% (municipalities); 20% (barangays) — percentages set by LGC §285.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Local Taxation
Post-*Mandanas*, IRA is now NTA computed on all national taxes — never apply the old internal-revenue-only base for NTA shares accruing after implementation. RPT is on assessed value (market value × assessment level) — never compute directly on market value. Specific LBT rates vary by LGU ordinance within LGC maximums — never cite a fixed rate without noting LGU variability. RPT delinquency interest cap is 36 months — do not apply interest beyond that cap. RPT exemption requires actual, direct, exclusive use — partial or incidental use disqualifies. The transfer tax power applies to all modes of real property transfer; it is separate from national capital gains tax and documentary stamp tax.

## Local Business Tax — Expanded Rules

**Withdrawal from place of production (§143[a])**: Manufacturers pay LBT on annual gross sales; LBT accrues at point of withdrawal from premises. Sales recorded in head office but goods withdrawn from branch: LBT due at branch location. *Distillers/fermenters*: LBT on value of goods at point of production.

**Service enterprises and contractors (§143[e])**: Gross receipts = amounts actually or constructively received; billings not yet collected not included until received. Distinguished from manufacturers — service businesses pay on receipts, not sales.

**Financial institutions (§143[f])**: Banks — on gross receipts from interest, commissions, discounts, fees; up to 50% of 1% on municipalities; up to 75% of 1% on cities. Pawnshops — 3% of gross receipts.

**Situs rules for multi-LGU businesses**:
- Recording of sales in head office: 30% to LGU where head office is; 70% allocated among branches in proportion to sales in each.
- No fixed place: 100% to principal office LGU.
- Salesmen covering multiple LGUs: allocated per actual sales records.

**Exemptions from LBT (§133)**: Goods and services produced by small-scale miners using actual artisanal mining; cooperatives (R.A. 6938); BOI-registered enterprises during ITH period; PEZA-registered enterprises within ecozones; senior citizen discounts (R.A. 9994).

## Real Property Tax — Expanded Rules

**Classification of land (§217)**: Agricultural; residential; commercial; industrial; mineral; timberland. Classification determines assessment level per local ordinance.

**Assessment levels (§218)**: Vary by LGU ordinance within LGC-set maximums: residential land — up to 20%; commercial/industrial land — up to 50%; agricultural — up to 40%. Buildings and other improvements assessed separately.

**Revision of assessments (§219)**: General revision of assessments every 3 years. Property owners notified; may appeal to Local Board of Assessment Appeals (LBAA) within 60 days from receipt of notice.

**Appeal process**: Taxpayer disagrees with assessment → file appeal with LBAA → appeal LBAA decision to Central Board of Assessment Appeals (CBAA) → appeal CBAA decision to CA via Rule 43. Tax must be paid under protest (§252) to maintain appeal.

**Payment under protest (§252)**: RPT must be paid first before assessment can be contested; annotation on receipt that payment is "under protest"; failure to pay under protest = deemed waiver of right to contest amount.

**Taxpayer's remedies**: (1) Protest of assessment with LBAA within 60 days; (2) Claim for refund/credit of erroneously/illegally paid RPT — file within 2 years from date of payment; (3) Challenge exemption denial before LBAA.

**Real property exempt from levy**: Real property of Republic, provinces, cities, municipalities; government-owned real property actually devoted to public use; property covered by pending constitutional challenge on taxation.

## Local Tax Ordinances — Procedure
**Enactment requirements**: Local tax ordinances passed by Sanggunian (local legislative body); public hearings required; ordinance takes effect upon posting or publication as required by law; published in LGU official bulletin board or local newspaper.

**Publication requirement**: Ordinances imposing taxes — posted for minimum period and published in the province/city/municipality's official bulletin board; in newspapers of general circulation in the LGU if available.

**Taxpayer challenges**: (1) Challenge validity of ordinance before RTC within 30 days from effectivity; OR (2) Challenge before DOJ Secretary within 30 days from effectivity on grounds of unconstitutionality or illegality. DOJ ruling appealed to RTC.

**Prescriptive periods for local tax assessment**: LGU may assess taxes within 5 years from date taxes became due; in case of fraud or intent to evade — 10 years from discovery. No prescription runs if taxpayer fails to file required return.

---

## Special Levies

**Socialized housing levy (§235)**: LGU may impose additional levy not exceeding 0.5% on assessed value of land in excess of ₱50,000 for socialized housing.

**Community tax (§§156–164)**: Imposed on: (1) natural persons — at least 18 years old who have been employed, engaged in business/occupation, or have real property with aggregate assessed value ≥ ₱1,000; (2) juridical persons doing business or with real property. Community tax certificate (cedula) required as identification for notarial acts, voter registration, and certain official transactions.

**Franchise taxes (§137)**: Provinces may impose franchise tax on franchise-holding businesses not exceeding 50% of 1% of gross annual receipts.

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Local Taxation Update
NTA (post-*Mandanas*) base = all national taxes, not just internal revenue collections — this significantly increased LGU allotments beginning FY2022. RPT payment under protest is mandatory — no exceptions; failure to pay first = waiver. LBAA/CBAA appeal deadlines are prescriptive — failure to file within 60 days from notice = assessment becomes final. LBT situs rules for multi-LGU businesses are complex; always check actual sales recording vs. delivery location. Local ordinance validity challenge has a strict 30-day filing period — courts may dismiss as time-barred. PEZA enterprises within ecozones are exempt from LBT and RPT (subject to 5% GIT); BOI enterprises are NOT automatically exempt from LBT/RPT. Senior citizen discounts are not optional for covered establishments — compliance is mandatory and non-compliance has penalties.

## LGU Fiscal Autonomy and Revenue Generation

**Constitutional basis (Art. X §5, 1987 Constitution)**: LGUs have power to create their own sources of revenue and levy taxes, fees, charges subject to guidelines and limitations by Congress. LGC implements this constitutional grant.

**Residual taxing power (§186 LGC)**: LGUs may exercise residual taxing power — levy taxes, fees, charges not otherwise specifically enumerated in LGC, or taxed under NIRC or other applicable laws. Residual power gives LGUs broad authority but cannot overlap with national taxes already imposed on the same subject.

**Principle of uniformity**: Local tax ordinances must apply uniformly throughout the LGU's jurisdiction; cannot single out particular businesses without rational basis.

---

## Business Permit and Licensing

**Mayor's permit / business permit**: Annual; prerequisite for doing business within the LGU. Fee components: registration fee (set by ordinance); sanitary/health inspection fee; fire inspection fee; zoning fee. Mayor's permit is a license, not a tax — but fees must not be excessive or confiscatory.

**Renewal deadline**: January 20 of each year (or as prescribed by local ordinance). Late renewal: surcharge up to 25% of fee plus interest.

**Revocation**: LGU may revoke business permit for violation of ordinances or public health/safety laws; notice and hearing required before revocation (due process).

---

## Tax Collection and Enforcement by LGUs

**Local treasurer's power**: Local treasurers collect local taxes; issue warrants of levy and distraint; conduct public auction of delinquent property. Procedures parallel to BIR collection but governed by LGC.

**Distraint of personal property**: Local treasurer may seize personal property of delinquent taxpayer; sale at public auction after proper notice; excess proceeds returned to taxpayer.

**Real property levy and auction**: RPT delinquency → levy annotated on title → notice to owner and publication → public auction; owner has right of redemption within 1 year from date of auction.

**Priority of LGU tax claims**: Local government taxes constitute a lien on property of taxpayer superior to other claims, except liens in favor of national government.

---

## Fiscal Transfers — Intergovernmental

**National Tax Allotment (NTA) — post-Mandanas implementation**:
- Base = all national taxes (not just internal revenue taxes).
- Includes: customs duties, documentary stamp tax, taxes from national agencies, etc.
- Phased implementation: FY2022 implementation; LGUs received significantly higher NTA starting 2022.
- Devolution: With higher NTA, LGUs expected to absorb devolved national agency functions (health centers, agricultural extension, social welfare services).

**Grants and special funds**: LGUs receive grants from national agencies for specific purposes (e.g., DILG performance challenge fund, MDFO loans). Subject to conditionalities and audit by COA.

**LGU borrowings**: LGUs may contract loans and issue bonds with national government guarantee or credit enhancement. Subject to DBM debt ceiling; Bangko Sentral approval for large borrowings.

---

## Assessment and Protest in Local Taxes

**Overpayment and refund**: Taxpayer may claim refund of overpaid local taxes within 2 years from date of payment. Claim filed with local treasurer; denial appealable to RTC.

**Question of LGU authority**: If local ordinance is challenged as beyond LGU's taxing power or in conflict with NIRC, challenge filed with RTC; not with BIR or CTA (CTA jurisdiction is limited to national taxes). *PLDT v. City of Davao* (GR 143867): National franchise tax paid under the franchise law does not preclude LGU local franchise tax unless the national law expressly exempts.

**Double taxation**: Not prohibited per se in the Philippines as between national and local taxes; prohibited only if same authority taxes the same subject twice for the same period and purpose (*Villanueva v. City of Iloilo*, GR L-26521).

---

## Key Cases — Local Taxation
**Batangas Power Corp. v. Batangas City** (GR 152675): PEZA-registered enterprises are exempt from all taxes including local business tax — the 5% gross income tax is in lieu of all national AND local taxes; LGUs cannot impose LBT on PEZA enterprises within ecozones.

**PLDT v. City of Davao** (GR 143867): City may impose local franchise tax even if national franchise tax has been paid — unless the national statute expressly grants exemption from local taxation; merely imposing a national tax does not preempt local taxation.

**Pelizloy Realty Corp. v. Province of Benguet** (GR 183137): Province may not impose local amusement tax on admission fees to swimming pools, resorts, tourist spots, and recreational facilities — these are NOT "places of amusement" under LGC; LGC specifically lists places of amusement; provinces cannot expand beyond the enumerated list.

**City of Manila v. Coca-Cola Bottlers Philippines** (GR 181845): City cannot impose local business tax on both manufacturers and retailers for the same goods — this constitutes double taxation prohibited under LGC; manufacturer who delivers goods directly to customers is a manufacturer, not a retailer, for LBT situs purposes.

## Miscellaneous Local Revenue Sources
**Stalls and market fees**: LGU-operated public markets may charge stall rental fees; separate from LBT; governed by market ordinance; rates set by Sanggunian. Public market operators (private concessionaires) paying LGU fixed fees may pass cost to stallholders.
**Building permit fees**: Collected by LGU building official; basis: cost of construction per National Building Code; not a tax but a regulatory fee; payable before construction commences.
**Barangay clearance fees**: Barangays charge fees for barangay business clearance, a prerequisite for city/municipal business permit; fees must be reasonable; barangay cannot impose LBT directly (no taxing power over businesses except as shared by the city/municipality).
**Environmental and development fees**: Some LGUs impose environmental fees (garbage collection, sewage treatment); development fees on subdivision projects; impact fees on commercial developments; legality depends on specific LGC authorization and ordinance basis.
**Penalty for non-payment of LBT**: Surcharge of 25% of unpaid tax; plus 2% monthly interest until paid; maximum 36 months of interest. After delinquency, civil action for collection or distraint/levy available to local treasurer.

**Miscellaneous LGU regulatory fees**: LGU may impose fees for permits, licenses, and regulatory approvals without them being classified as taxes — provided fees are commensurate with cost of regulation. Excessive fees that bear no reasonable relationship to regulatory cost are void as disguised taxes beyond LGU authority (*Palma Development Corp. v. Malangas*, GR 152492).
