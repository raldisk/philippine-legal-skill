# Income Taxation

## Primary Sources
**NIRC (R.A. 8424) as amended by TRAIN (R.A. 10963, 2018) and CREATE (R.A. 11534, 2021)** — Title II governs income tax. **BIR** — Revenue Regulations, RMOs, RMCs implement NIRC. BIR issuances are binding on taxpayers and BIR officers but subject to judicial review.

---

## Taxable Income — Core Concepts
**Gross income (§32)**: All income from whatever source — compensation; business/profession income; gains from property; interest; rents; royalties; dividends; annuities; prizes/winnings; pensions; partner's distributive share.

**Exclusions (§32[B])**: Life insurance proceeds on death; gifts/bequests/devises; compensation for personal injuries; income exempt by treaty; retirement benefits under R.A. 7641 or qualified private plans; SSS/GSIS/PhilHealth benefits; 13th month pay and other benefits not exceeding ₱90,000.

---

## Individual Income Tax — TRAIN Graduated Rates (2023 onwards)
| Taxable Income | Tax |
|---|---|
| Not over ₱250,000 | Exempt |
| ₱250,001–₱400,000 | 15% on excess over ₱250,000 |
| ₱400,001–₱800,000 | ₱22,500 + 20% on excess over ₱400,000 |
| ₱800,001–₱2,000,000 | ₱102,500 + 25% on excess over ₱800,000 |
| ₱2,000,001–₱8,000,000 | ₱402,500 + 30% on excess over ₱2,000,000 |
| Over ₱8,000,000 | ₱2,202,500 + 35% on excess over ₱8,000,000 |

**Minimum wage earners**: Exempt from income tax on statutory minimum wage including holiday pay, OT pay, night shift differential, and hazard pay.

**Deductions**: Itemized (expenses, interest, taxes, losses, bad debts, depreciation, charitable contributions) OR Optional Standard Deduction (OSD) at 40% of gross sales/receipts for individuals.

## Corporate Income Tax — CREATE
**Domestic corps (worldwide income)**:
- RCIT: 25% (large corps); 20% for domestic corps with net taxable income ≤ ₱5M AND total assets ≤ ₱100M (excluding land).
- MCIT: 2% of gross income; applies from 4th taxable year; 1% under CREATE for 2020–2022; restored 2%.
- When MCIT > RCIT → pay MCIT. Excess MCIT carried forward 3 years as credit against RCIT.

**Resident foreign corps**: Philippine-source income only; same 25% RCIT.

**Non-resident foreign corps**: 25% final tax on gross Philippine-source income.

**Special rates**: Proprietary educational institutions/non-profit hospitals — 10%; international carriers — 2.5% on gross Philippine billings; REITs — verify current rules.

---

## Passive Income — Final Taxes
| Income Type | Resident | Non-resident |
|---|---|---|
| Bank deposits/deposit substitutes | 20% final | 25% final |
| Dividends from domestic corp | 10% final | 25% final |
| Intercorporate dividends (dom-to-dom) | Exempt | — |
| Royalties | 20% (books/music: 10%) | 25% |
| Long-term deposits ≥5 years (individuals) | Exempt | — |

**Capital gains — real property (capital asset)**: 6% CGT on gross selling price or FMV, whichever is higher. Distinguished from ordinary assets (subject to regular income tax).

**Capital gains — shares not traded on PSE**: 15% CGT.

---

## Withholding Tax System
**Creditable withholding tax (CWT)**: Withheld at source; creditable against payee's income tax liability. Used for professional fees, rentals, commissions, contractor payments.

**Final withholding tax (FWT)**: Full settlement; no further return by payee. Used for passive income items above.

**Compensation withholding**: Employers withhold monthly using BIR prescribed tables; remit within deadline.

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Income Taxation
Never apply pre-TRAIN graduated rates to income earned after January 1, 2018. Never apply 30% RCIT to any corporation without checking CREATE's 25%/20% rates — 30% is the pre-CREATE rate. MCIT rate was temporarily reduced to 1% for 2020–2022 under CREATE — use 2% outside that period. 6% CGT applies to capital assets only — ordinary assets are subject to regular income tax; always determine asset classification. Intercorporate dividends between domestic corporations are tax-exempt. The ₱250,000 exemption for individuals is only for the graduated income tax table — other taxes (passive income FWTs) have separate rates. Never state that the minimum wage exemption covers all income of minimum wage earners — only the statutory minimum wage and the specific supplemental pays listed are exempt.

## Individual Taxation — Special Rules

**Mixed income earners**: Individuals earning both compensation income and business/professional income — compensation taxed at graduated rates; business/professional income may be subject to 8% flat tax option (below ₱3M gross sales/receipts, in lieu of graduated rates + percentage tax) OR graduated rates.

**8% Flat Tax Option**: Available to self-employed individuals and professionals with gross sales/receipts not exceeding ₱3M VAT threshold; elected annually on first income tax return; covers income from business and practice of profession; exclusive of income subject to final tax. Cannot be combined with itemized deductions.

**Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs)**: Non-resident citizens taxed only on Philippine-source income. OCW/OFW income from abroad is exempt from Philippine income tax.

**Alien individuals**: Resident aliens taxed on Philippine-source income at graduated rates. Non-resident aliens engaged in trade/business: graduated rates on net Philippine income. Non-resident aliens NOT engaged: 25% final tax on gross Philippine income.

## Corporate Income Tax — Specific Rules

**MCIT computation**: 2% × gross income (net sales minus cost of goods sold/services; NOT including deductions). Designed as minimum floor to prevent sustained zero-tax reporting through expense manipulation.

**Deferred MCIT credit**: Excess MCIT over RCIT carried forward and credited against RCIT for 3 immediately succeeding taxable years; not a refund.

**Improperly Accumulated Earnings Tax (IAET)**: 10% surcharge on improper accumulation of earnings beyond 100% of paid-up capital without justification. Only for domestic corporations; not for banks, insurance companies, publicly listed companies, and certain other entities.

**Income of offshore banking units (OBUs) and foreign currency deposit units (FCDUs)**: Income from foreign currency transactions exempt from income tax; domestic currency transactions subject to regular rates.

**Gross Philippine Billings (GPB)**: International carriers taxed at 2.5% on GPB — gross revenue from uplift of passengers/cargo in Philippines, including amounts paid outside Philippines to the extent attributable to Philippines segment.

---

## Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT)

**Coverage**: Fringe benefits furnished to managerial/supervisory employees (not rank-and-file; rank-and-file fringe benefits are part of gross compensation). Employer pays FBT; deductible from employer's gross income.

**Rate**: 35% of grossed-up monetary value of fringe benefit. Grossed-up: divide actual benefit by (1 − 0.35) = 1.5385 × benefit.

**Exempt fringe benefits**: Benefits authorized and exempted under special laws; contributions to retirement/insurance/hospitalization benefit plans; benefits given for convenience of employer; de minimis benefits within BIR-prescribed limits; 13th month pay and other benefits not exceeding ₱90,000 annual cap.

**De minimis benefits (RR 11-2018)**: Monetized leave (up to 10 days); medical cash allowance to dependents (₱1,500/semester); rice subsidy (₱2,000/month or 50kg rice equivalent); uniform/clothing allowance; actual medical assistance; laundry allowance (₱300/month); employee achievement awards; Christmas/major anniversary gifts (₱5,000/year); daily meal allowance for OT/graveyard (₱25% of minimum wage).

## Capital Gains Tax — Detailed Rules

**Real property CGT (§24[D])**: 6% of higher of: (1) gross selling price; (2) FMV at time of sale (higher of zonal value or assessor's FMV). Applies to capital assets only. Ordinary assets (used in trade/business, inventory): regular income tax at graduated/RCIT rate.

**Shares CGT (§24[C])**: 15% on net gain (SP less cost) for shares of stock in a domestic corporation NOT listed on PSE.

**Installment sale of real property**: If initial payment ≤ 25% of GSP, tax may be paid in installments corresponding to installment payments received.

**Principal residence exemption (§24[D][2])**: CGT on sale of principal residence exempt IF: entire proceeds used to acquire or construct new principal residence within 18 months from sale; CIR notified within 30 days from sale; exemption available only once every 10 years.

---

## PEZA and BOI Incentives

**PEZA-registered enterprises (R.A. 7916)**: Income tax holiday (ITH) for 4–6 years; after ITH, 5% gross income tax (GIT) in lieu of all national and local taxes on registered activities. Under CREATE (R.A. 11534) and CITIRA regime — new entrants: ITH for certain years then enhanced deductions or 10% special corporate income tax (SCIT).

**BOI-registered enterprises (EO 226)**: ITH for 3–6 years depending on pioneer/non-pioneer status; various additional deductions; no GIT option (unlike PEZA). Under CREATE: transitional provisions for existing registrants.

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Income Taxation Update
8% flat tax option available only to individual taxpayers (not corporations) with gross sales below ₱3M — not available if sales exceed threshold or taxpayer is VAT-registered. MCIT of 2% applies from 4th taxable year; CREATE temporarily reduced to 1% (2020–2022); now restored to 2%. Never apply IAET to banks, insurance companies, or listed companies. OFW income from abroad is exempt — only Philippine-source income of non-resident citizens is taxable. FBT is paid by the employer, not the employee. Principal residence exemption is available once every 10 years — verify last use. PEZA GIT of 5% is under review/transition under CREATE — always check current status of registrant's ITH period.

## Deductions from Gross Income — Detailed Rules

**Itemized deductions (§34 NIRC)**: Available to individuals and corporations in business or practice of profession; not available where final tax applies or where OSD elected.

**Interest expense (§34[B])**: Deductible if paid or incurred in connection with taxpayer's trade, business, or profession; on indebtedness. TRAIN limitation: reduce deductible interest by 33% (individuals) of interest income subject to final tax. Limitation prevents arbitrage between taxable interest expense and tax-exempt interest income.

**Taxes (§34[C])**: Taxes paid or incurred during taxable year in trade or business — deductible (except income tax, estate tax, donor's tax, special assessments, VAT). Foreign income taxes: taxpayer may claim either as a credit (form 1702-RT) OR as a deduction — not both.

**Losses (§34[D])**: Losses from fire, storm, shipwreck, other casualties, or robbery/theft that are: (1) incurred in trade/business/profession; (2) not compensated by insurance; (3) not claimed as estate tax deduction. Net operating loss carryover (NOLCO): 3 years. CREATE provides 5-year NOLCO for losses during 2020–2021 taxable years.

**Bad debts (§34[E])**: Bona fide debt arising from business; ascertained worthless during taxable year; actually charged off. Subsequent recovery: included in income when recovered (tax benefit rule).

**Depreciation (§34[F])**: Reasonable allowance for exhaustion, wear and tear, obsolescence of property used in trade/business/profession. Methods: straight-line; declining balance; sum of years digits; other method approved by BIR. NOLCO and depreciation interact — verify treatment when depreciation creates a loss.

**Charitable contributions and other deductions (§34[H])**: Donations to domestic corporations organized exclusively for education/science/culture/etc.: deductible up to 10% of taxable income (individuals) or 5% (corporations). Priority/accredited donees: unlimited deduction. Verify BIR accreditation of receiving entity.

## Optional Standard Deduction (OSD)

**For individuals (§34[L])**: 40% of gross sales/receipts; in lieu of all itemized deductions and personal exemptions; elected at filing of 1st quarterly return and irrevocable for the year.

**For corporations (§34[L])**: 40% of gross income; gross income = net sales minus cost of goods sold/services; in lieu of itemized deductions.

**When OSD preferred**: When actual deductible expenses < 40% of gross income; when records are insufficient to support itemized deductions; when simplicity of filing is desired.

## Tax Treaties — Income Tax

**Reduced withholding rates**: Tax treaties generally reduce Philippine withholding rates on: dividends (5–15% depending on treaty vs. 25% regular); royalties (10–15% vs. 20–25% regular); interest (10–15% vs. 20–25% regular); service fees (0–10% vs. regular CWT).

**Treaty eligibility**: Beneficial owner must be a resident of the treaty partner; tax treaty relief applications (TTRA) filed with BIR International Tax Affairs Division (ITAD) before or concurrent with transaction. *CIR v. S.C. Johnson* (GR 127105): Taxpayer must be the beneficial owner, not a mere conduit; treaty shopping disallowed.

**Most-favored-nation (MFN) clause**: In tax treaties providing MFN, if the Philippines gives a more favorable rate to a third country, the same rate applies to the treaty partner. Philippine courts have upheld MFN claims (*CIR v. Procter & Gamble Philippine Manufacturing*, GR 66838).

## Reporting and Filing

**Individual income tax returns**: BIR Form 1701 (individuals in business/profession); 1701A (individuals with no other income, pure compensation or OSD election); filed April 15 (April 15 = final deadline; quarterly installments required).

**Corporate income tax returns**: BIR Form 1702 series; fiscal year filers: 15th day of 4th month after end of fiscal year; quarterly returns due.

**Withholding tax returns**: Monthly (BIR Form 0619-E, 0619-F); quarterly (BIR Form 1601-EQ, 1601-FQ).

**Electronic filing**: BIR eFPS (Electronic Filing and Payment System) mandatory for large taxpayers, top-grossing businesses, and government agencies; penalty for non-use when mandatory.

## Key Cases — Income Taxation
**Madrigal v. Rafferty** (GR L-12287): Income is the fruit that separates from the capital; capital itself is not income. Foundational case on income vs. return of capital distinction.

**CIR v. Filinvest Development Corp.** (GR 163653): Advances by parent corporation to its subsidiaries without interest — CIR may impute interest income (§50 NIRC arm's length standard); interest-free loans between related parties may constitute taxable benefit.

**Commissioner v. CA and YMCA** (GR 124043 consolidated): YMCA's rental income from commercial establishments and parking fees are subject to income tax even if YMCA is a non-profit organization — exemptions are strictly construed; income-producing activities outside the primary exempt purpose are taxable.

**Gorospe v. Commissioner** (GR L-25074): Retirement benefits from a private benefit plan qualify for tax exemption only if the plan meets NIRC requirements — tenure and age requirements must be satisfied.

## Special Topics — Income Tax
**Imputed income**: Philippine income tax does not generally tax imputed income (e.g., value of owner-occupied home, services performed for oneself). Exceptions: §50 related-party adjustments; BIR may impute income on below-market loans between related parties.
**Crop year basis for farmers**: Individual farmers may use crop year method of accounting — income and expenses recognized per crop cycle rather than calendar year; must be consistently applied.
**Cancellation of indebtedness**: When debt is cancelled by creditor — generally taxable income to debtor (NIRC §32[A]); exception: capital contribution by stockholder-creditor to the debtor-corporation treated as equity infusion, not income.
**Tax benefit rule**: When a previously deducted item is later recovered (bad debt recovery; refund of deducted tax) — amount recovered is income in year of recovery to the extent the deduction created a tax benefit.
**Below-market sales**: Sale of property at below-market price to related party — difference between market price and consideration: (a) if employer to employee: compensation income to employee; (b) if between family members: gift subject to donor's tax; (c) between related corporations: arm's-length adjustment under §50.
