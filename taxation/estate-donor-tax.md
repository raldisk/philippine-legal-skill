# Estate and Donor's Tax

## Primary Sources
**NIRC Title III (§§84–104) as amended by TRAIN (R.A. 10963, 2018)** — estate tax (§§84–97) and donor's tax (§§98–104). TRAIN introduced flat rates and revised deductions. Rates below apply to deaths/donations on or after January 1, 2018.

---

## Estate Tax
**Nature**: Excise tax on the privilege of transmitting property upon death. Imposed on the net estate of the decedent — NOT directly on heirs.

**TRAIN flat rate**: **6% of net estate exceeding ₱5 million standard deduction.**

**Gross estate**:
- Citizens/residents: all property worldwide.
- Non-resident aliens: Philippine-situated property only.
- Includes: decedent's interest in property; transfers in contemplation of death; revocable transfers; property passing under general power of appointment; life insurance proceeds (where estate is named beneficiary or decedent retained incidents of ownership); prior interests.

**Deductions from gross estate (TRAIN)**:
| Deduction | Amount |
|---|---|
| Standard deduction | ₱5,000,000 (automatic — no documentation needed) |
| Family home | FMV up to ₱10,000,000 |
| Ordinary deductions | Losses, indebtedness, taxes accrued before death, transfers for public use |
| Benefits under R.A. 4917 | Retirement benefits from employer |
| Medical expenses | Incurred within 1 year before death; up to ₱500,000 |
| Surviving spouse's share | Excluded (not part of decedent's estate) |

**Filing and payment**: Estate tax return due **within 1 year from date of death**. CIR may extend up to 2 years for meritorious reasons. Heirs personally liable for estate tax to the extent of property received.

**Estate Tax Amnesty (R.A. 11213 as amended)**: For decedents dying on or before December 31, 2017. Verify current availability and deadline — amnesty periods are subject to extension by legislation.

---

## Donor's Tax
**Nature**: Tax on gratuitous inter vivos transfers.

**TRAIN flat rate**: **6% of net donations exceeding ₱250,000 during the calendar year.**

**Applies to**: Citizens/residents on worldwide donations; non-residents on Philippine-situated property.

**Tax-exempt donations**:
- Gifts to national government or government entities created by law.
- Gifts to accredited educational, charitable, religious, cultural, social welfare, or non-profit institutions — provided not more than 30% is used for administration.
- Gifts between spouses — but property regime governs what is separately owned; verify.

**Filing**: Donor's tax return due **within 30 days after the gift is made**. Multiple donations within the year: cumulative total determines rate application.

**Valuation**: Personal property — FMV at time of gift. Real property — higher of zonal value or assessor's FMV.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Estate and Donor's Tax
Never apply pre-TRAIN graduated estate/donor's tax rates to deaths/donations after January 1, 2018. Pre-TRAIN standard deduction was ₱1M and family home deduction was ₱1M — both are now ₱5M and ₱10M respectively under TRAIN. Estate tax is on the estate, not the heirs directly — heirs' liability is limited to value of property received. The 1-year filing period runs from the date of death; do not apply it from date of settlement. Donor's tax threshold of ₱250,000 is cumulative within the calendar year — each donation within the year adds to the running total. Estate Tax Amnesty coverage and deadline must be verified against current BIR issuances — the amnesty has been extended multiple times.

## Estate Tax — Detailed Rules

**Gross estate inclusions — expanded**:
- **Transfers in contemplation of death**: Transfers made within 3 years before death are presumed in contemplation of death and included in gross estate unless rebutted. Gift taxes paid on these transfers are allowed as deduction.
- **Revocable transfers**: Transfers where decedent retained power to alter, amend, revoke, or terminate the enjoyment without beneficiary consent — fully included.
- **General power of appointment**: Property over which decedent had unilateral power to appoint to self, estate, creditors, or creditors of estate — included regardless of whether exercised.
- **Life insurance**: Proceeds includible if: (1) estate is named beneficiary, or (2) decedent retained incidents of ownership (right to change beneficiary; right to surrender or cancel; right to pledge). If irrevocable third-party beneficiary — excluded from estate.
- **Conjugal/community property**: Only decedent's share (1/2 of net conjugal/community property, unless contrary to marriage settlement) is included.

**Valuation rules**:
- Real property: Higher of zonal value (BIR RDO-determined) or assessor's FMV at time of death.
- Personal property (cash): Face value.
- Shares of stock (listed): Mean of highest and lowest trading price on death date; shares not listed: book value based on audited FS.
- Receivables: Present value if not matured; doubtful accounts — estimated collectible amount.
- Foreign property: Fair market value in foreign jurisdiction, converted at BSP rate at death.

**Deductions — additional rules**:
- **Losses**: Casualty, robbery, theft losses incurred during settlement; not compensated by insurance; claims within 1 year from death date.
- **Indebtedness**: Valid debts of decedent existing at death; not condoned; contracted in good faith; with supporting documents; mortgages included only to extent property is included in gross estate.
- **Taxes accrued before death**: Taxes owed by decedent at time of death; not penalties/surcharges accruing after.
- **Transfer for public use**: Amount in decedent's will for public purpose (government, charitable institution).

**Filing requirements**:
- Form BIR 1801 (Estate Tax Return).
- Attachments: certified true copy of death certificate; deed of partition; proof of deductions; TIN of decedent and heirs.
- Cash and/or installment payment options available (if estate cash insufficient — installment over 2 years without interest/surcharge, with BIR approval).

## Donor's Tax — Detailed Rules

**Cumulative computation**: All donations within the calendar year aggregated. First ₱250,000 of total yearly gifts is exempt. Rate of 6% applies to cumulative excess. Each donation in the year: compute cumulative total; subtract prior tax paid; pay balance.

**Valuation of gifts**:
- Real property: Higher of zonal value or assessor's FMV at date of gift.
- Personal property: FMV at date of gift.
- Services: Not subject to donor's tax — requires economic benefit transfer.
- Condonation of debt: Subject to donor's tax — donor is the creditor; gift is the value of condonation.

**Inadequate consideration**: If consideration received is less than FMV of property transferred, the difference is a gift subject to donor's tax. *Exception*: transactions in ordinary course of business at arm's length — not a gift even if below FMV.

**Transfers that are NOT gifts (no donor's tax)**:
- Transfers under fiduciary obligation.
- Transfers for adequate and full consideration in money or money's worth.
- Renunciation of share by heir in intestate succession — not subject if general and unconditional.
- Transfers required by court decree.

**Splitting of gift**: If spouses split gifts, each spouse reports half; both file separate donor's tax returns.

**Government donations**: Donation to national/local government for public purpose — exempt from donor's tax AND deductible from donor's income tax (subject to limitations).

## Relationship Between Estate and Donor's Taxes
**Gift-in-contemplation-of-death**: Brought back into gross estate; gift tax paid credited against estate tax due.

**Gifts within 3 years of death**: Create presumption of contemplation — practical trap for estate planners. Rebuttable only by clear evidence of independent non-death-related motivation.

**Succession planning considerations**: Philippine law limits aggressive estate planning (revocable trusts included in estate; no step-up in basis at death under NIRC). Irrevocable life insurance trust (ILIT) and BIR-compliant family foundations are tools — each has specific NIRC and BIR circular requirements.

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Update
TRAIN reduced standard deduction from ₱1M to ₱5M (increased); family home deduction from ₱1M to ₱10M (increased). Never apply pre-TRAIN graduated estate tax table to post-2018 deaths. Transfers in contemplation of death have 3-year presumption — this is a rebuttable presumption. Estate tax amnesty for pre-2018 deaths — check current extension status under BIR issuances. Donor's tax exemption of ₱250,000 is cumulative annual, not per-gift. Condonation of debt is a taxable gift — not a simple forgiveness with no tax consequence. General power of appointment property is included in estate even if the power is never exercised.

## Gross Estate — Specific Inclusions Deep Dive

**Property passing under general power of appointment**: Decedent who during lifetime holds power to appoint property to self, estate, creditors, or estate's creditors — the appointable property is part of decedent's gross estate. Limited power of appointment (can only appoint to a defined class, excluding self and estate) — NOT included.

**Proceeds of life insurance — policy details**:
- Named beneficiary is irrevocable third-party: excluded from gross estate even if decedent paid premiums.
- Named beneficiary is revocable third-party: included — decedent retained incidents (right to change beneficiary).
- Named beneficiary is estate: included.
- Policy assigned absolutely to another during lifetime: not included if decedent retained no incidents.

**Accrued income up to death**: Salary earned but not yet paid; dividends declared but not yet distributed if record date precedes death; accrued interest on deposits — all included.

## Administration Expenses as Deductions

**Ordinary deductions (§86[A][3] NIRC)**:
- Funeral expenses: actual amount up to ₱200,000 (pre-TRAIN cap); under TRAIN, absorbed into standard deduction — no longer a separate line item. Verify current BIR ruling.
- Judicial/extrajudicial settlement costs: attorney's fees, court costs, accountant's fees for estate administration — deductible if necessary and not excessive.
- Claims against estate: debts contracted in good faith during decedent's lifetime; notarized for debts over ₱5,000; supported by vouchers; not condoned.

**Claims against insolvent persons**: Amount included in gross estate (accounts receivable from insolvent debtor); deductible to the extent uncollectible — requires proof of insolvency of the debtor.

## Tax Treaties — Estate and Gift Aspects

**US-Philippines Tax Treaty**: Provides relief for US citizens or Philippine citizens with US estate tax exposure; estate tax credits for double taxation. Requires careful analysis where decedent had dual citizenship or assets in both countries.

**OECD Model**: Philippines not an OECD member but follows some treaty conventions in bilateral tax treaties; most PH treaties focus on income, not estate/gift tax.

**Treaty override**: Philippine domestic law governs unless treaty expressly provides more favorable treatment; treaties self-execute under the Philippine Constitution.

## Estate Settlement Procedure — Tax Compliance

**Extrajudicial settlement (R.A. 7662/Rule 74, Rules of Court)**: Heirs may settle estate outside court if: no will; no debts; all heirs are of legal age or minors represented by judicial guardians; divide estate by affidavit executed by all heirs; published in newspaper of general circulation once a week for 3 consecutive weeks.

**BIR clearance before distribution**: Heirs cannot receive property from the estate until the estate tax has been paid and BIR issues estate tax clearance (eCAR — electronic Certificate Authorizing Registration). No Register of Deeds, LTO, or similar registry may transfer title without eCAR.

**Multiple executors/administrators**: If more than one, all are jointly and severally liable for estate tax. Personal liability arises from distributing assets before paying estate tax.

## Donor's Tax — Corporate Donations

**Donation by corporation**: Corporation may donate if within its powers (AOI purpose, or expressly authorized). Donation to accredited non-profit organization: deductible from corporate income tax up to 10% of taxable income (individuals: 10% of net income).

**Donation of shares**: FMV of shares at time of donation; use book value (audited FS) for unlisted shares; for listed shares — mean of highest and lowest price on date of donation.

**Donation mortis causa**: A donation that takes effect at the time of the donor's death is, in reality, a testamentary disposition — subject to estate tax, not donor's tax. The label "donation" does not govern; the operative trigger (death) determines tax.

## Key Cases — Estate and Donor's Tax
**CIR v. Estate of Benigno Toda Jr.** (GR 147188): Estate is not liable for income tax generated by the taxpayer's tax evasion scheme that was actually a disguised sale — BUT the scheme was recharacterized. The boundary between estate's liability and the decedent's personal tax liability is factual.

**Vda. de Roces v. Posadas** (GR L-34937): Life insurance proceeds where the decedent retained the right to change the beneficiary are included in the gross estate — the irrevocability of beneficiary designation is the controlling fact.

**Gabriel v. CA** (GR L-43735): Valuation of estate property at zonal value or assessor's fair market value must be at the DATE OF DEATH — not the date of filing or date of settlement.

**Important computation note**: When both conjugal and exclusive property exist, compute surviving spouse's share of conjugal property FIRST; only the decedent's share (exclusive + ½ conjugal net of conjugal debts) forms the gross estate. Never include surviving spouse's ½ share in the taxable estate.

## Practical Estate Tax Planning Notes
**Survivorship accounts**: Joint accounts with right of survivorship — BIR position: 50% of balance presumed part of decedent's estate; taxpayer may rebut with proof of actual contribution.
**Peso-denominated FCDU deposits**: Included in gross estate at peso equivalent; not subject to bank secrecy for estate tax assessment purposes.
**Real property valuation disputes**: If BIR zonal value is contested, taxpayer may present independent appraisal; BIR not bound by independent appraisal but court may consider it in tax protest.
**Agricultural land deduction**: Agricultural land devoted to productive purposes may qualify for deduction if within the applicable family home value or ordinary deduction categories — verify with current BIR rulings.
**Estate with pending litigation**: Estate property subject to litigation: include full value in gross estate; deductible as claim against estate only after final adjudication; conservative approach is to include without deduction and file amended return when resolved.
**Multiple deaths (commorientes)**: If decedent and heir die in same disaster without survivorship established — each estate computed separately; property that would have passed to predeceased heir forms part of decedent's estate.

**Filing extensions**: CIR may extend estate tax return deadline for up to 2 years for meritorious cases (e.g., estate under litigation; property located abroad; complex valuation disputes). Extension request filed before original 1-year deadline. Interest continues to run during extension period on unpaid tax.

**Substitution of executor**: If executor or administrator dies or becomes incapacitated during administration, a replacement is appointed by the probate court; the replacement inherits all tax obligations of the estate; personal liability attaches to the extent of estate property received.

**Heirs' tax obligations post-settlement**: Each heir is personally liable for estate tax to the extent of the value of the property received. If estate is distributed before estate tax is paid, each heir bears the proportionate burden — BIR can collect from individual heirs even after partition and distribution is complete.
