# Securities Law

## Primary Sources
**Securities Regulation Code (R.A. 8799)** — issuance/sale/trading of securities; intermediary regulation; insider trading; market manipulation; tender offers; proxy. **SRC-IRR** — implementing rules. **PSE Listing Rules** — listed companies. **SEC Memorandum Circulars** — quasi-legislative.

---

## Definition of Securities
**§3.1 SRC**: Shares, participations, or interests in a corporation or commercial enterprise evidenced by any instrument, including: shares of stock; bonds; debentures; notes; evidences of indebtedness; asset-backed securities; investment contracts; participation certificates; proprietary shares; certificates of deposit for future subscriptions.

**Investment contract test (Howey, as applied in PH)**: (1) investment of money; (2) in a common enterprise; (3) expectation of profits; (4) solely or substantially from efforts of others. Applied by SEC against pyramid/investment scheme products.

**Exempt securities (§9)**: Government securities; BSP-supervised bank securities; foreign government securities (treaty); short-term commercial papers.

---

## Registration
**Mandatory**: No security may be offered or sold unless registered with SEC, or exempt under §9 or §10.

**Registration statement**: Prospectus (delivered to purchasers); audited financials; business description; directors/officers; use of proceeds; risk factors.

**Exempt transactions (§10)**: Private placements (≤ 20 offerees); sales to directors/officers/existing stockholders; sales to ≤ 20 persons in 12 months; sales to accredited investors; institutional transactions. Exemption covers registration only — anti-fraud provisions still apply.

---

## Disclosure — Public Companies
**Public company threshold**: Assets > ₱50M AND ≥ 200 shareholders each holding ≥ 100 shares.

**Periodic reports**: Annual (Form 17-A); Quarterly (Form 17-Q); Current/material event (Form 17-C).

**Materiality**: Information is material if a reasonable investor would consider it important in a buy/sell/hold decision, or if disclosure would reasonably affect market price.

---

## Prohibited Acts
**Insider trading (§27)**: Unlawful for an insider (director, officer, agent, employee, or any person with material non-public information) to trade securities of the issuer while aware of MNPI. Tipper-tippee liability: tippee who trades knowing information came from a breach of duty is liable. Both civil and criminal liability.

**Market manipulation (§26)**: Creating false/misleading appearance of active trading; artificially raising or depressing prices. Wash sales, matched orders, circulation of false information.

**Fraud (§26.1)**: Employing any device/scheme to defraud; obtaining money/property through material misstatements; operating any fraud in connection with securities.

---

## Tender Offers
**Mandatory tender offer (§19, SRC-IRR)**: Person or group acquiring ≥ 35% of voting equity of a public company must make a tender offer for remaining shares. Applies to creeping acquisitions.

**Procedure**: File tender offer statement with SEC; target company files recommendation statement; minimum 20 business-day offer period.

---

## Broker-Dealers and Intermediaries
**Registration required**: Broker-dealers, investment advisers, transfer agents. Must satisfy "fit and proper" requirements and minimum net capital.

**Suitability**: Recommendations must match client's risk profile, investment objective, and financial capacity.

**SRO**: PSE is a self-regulatory organization; rules subject to SEC oversight.

---

## Enforcement
**Civil liability (§61)**: Offer/sell unregistered securities or via material misstatement → liable to purchaser for consideration paid + interest − dividends received.

**Criminal liability (§73)**: Fine up to ₱5M and/or imprisonment up to 21 years.

**Prescriptive period**: Civil — 2 years from discovery, not later than 5 years from violation.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Securities Law
Not all securities require registration — §9 exempt securities and §10 exempt transactions are major carve-outs. Howey test is for investment contracts; do not apply mechanically without checking Philippine SC/SEC application. Insider trading reaches tippees only when they knew the information came from a breach of duty. Mandatory tender offer threshold (35%) is set by SRC-IRR — subject to SEC amendment; verify current figure. Public company thresholds (₱50M assets, 200 shareholders) are subject to SEC amendment. Civil prescriptive period: 2 years from discovery, outer limit 5 years — these are separate periods, both must be satisfied.
---

## Registration Process — Detail
**Filing**: Registration statement filed with SEC; includes prospectus, audited FS (2 years), articles and by-laws, material contracts, legal opinion, underwriting agreement.

**Review period**: SEC reviews within 45 days; may issue deficiency letter requiring amendment. Effective date: when SEC issues order of effectiveness. Preliminary prospectus may be distributed during review (not a binding offer).

**Due diligence defense**: Underwriters and other signatories may escape civil liability by proving they exercised reasonable care and had no reason to believe statements were false/misleading at time of effectiveness.

---

## Investor Protection — Prospectus Requirements
**Prospectus delivery**: Must be delivered before or at time of sale. Failure to deliver = rescission right for buyer. Prospectus must not omit material facts even if not expressly required to be disclosed.

**Preliminary prospectus ("red herring")**: Used during SEC review; must state prominently not yet effective; no binding sale during review period.

**Supplemental prospectus**: Filed for material events after effectiveness; must be delivered to investors before they are bound.

---

## Exempt Transactions — Detailed Analysis
**§10.1(a) Private placement (≤20 persons)**: No advertising; no general solicitation; offerees must be specifically identified. Key: offer, not sale, must be ≤20. Offerees who do not purchase still count.

**§10.1(l) Accredited investor**: Investors with sufficient financial sophistication or capacity (minimum net worth or income thresholds); defined in SRC-IRR. Corporate accredited investors: institutions, banks, insurance companies.

**§10.2 Resale restrictions**: Securities sold under exempt transaction have transfer restrictions for one year from last sale; resale during restriction period is a violation unless registered or another exemption applies.

---

## Insider Trading — Technical Analysis
**Who is an insider (§27.2)**: Director; officer; person with 10%+ beneficial ownership; person who learns MNPI through business relationship with issuer; government employee who learns MNPI in performance of duties; person who learns MNPI from any of the foregoing.

**MNPI elements**: (1) not generally available to the public; (2) if generally available, would be likely to affect price.

**Safe harbor — public disclosure**: Once information is fully and fairly disclosed to the public through proper channels (e.g., Form 17-C filing) and sufficient time has passed for the market to absorb it, information is no longer "non-public."

**Tipper-tippee chain**: Each tippee in the chain who receives MNPI and trades is liable if: (1) tippee knew the tipper breached a duty; (2) tippee benefited the tipper directly or indirectly.

**Civil liability (§27.1)**: Insider liable to person who simultaneously traded with the insider without knowledge of MNPI — disgorgement of profits or losses avoided; plus damages.

---

## Short Selling and Market Manipulation
**Short selling**: Selling securities one does not own; must be covered by borrowed shares. BSP/SEC rules govern disclosure of short positions.

**Wash sales**: Simultaneous purchases and sales by same beneficial owner to create appearance of volume; illegal market manipulation.

**Matched orders**: Pre-arranged trades between parties to create artificial volume or price; illegal.

**Squeezing**: Dominant party controls enough supply of a security or deliverable to force short sellers to cover at disadvantageous prices; illegal in extreme forms.

---

## Mandatory Tender Offer — Detailed Rules
**Threshold (SRC-IRR §19)**: Acquisition of 35% or more of voting equity of a public company; also applies to acquisition of 35% or more in one transaction or in a series of transactions within a 12-month period.

**Creeping acquisition**: If acquirer already holds 35%+ and will acquire an additional 1%+ within 12 months → another mandatory tender offer.

**Exemptions**: Privatization; court-approved restructuring; involuntary transfers; intra-group reorganizations subject to SEC approval.

**Tender offer requirements**: Minimum offer period 20 business days; payment within 10 days of expiration; offeror may not purchase except through the tender offer; best price rule — all tendering stockholders get the same price.

---

## Proxy Solicitation
**Public companies**: Any solicitation of 10+ stockholder proxies for listed company requires proxy statement filed with SEC and disclosed to PSE. Must contain material information for stockholder decision-making.

**Proxy contest**: Dissident soliciting proxies in opposition to management must file competing proxy materials with SEC.

---

## SRO and PSE Jurisdiction
**PSE as SRO**: Listing rules, trading rules, surveillance, and enforcement of market integrity obligations. PSE rules approved by SEC; SEC has oversight over PSE. Disputes between PSE and listed companies: SEC has appellate jurisdiction.

**Broker-dealer capital requirements**: Minimum net capital (haircut method); quarterly FOCUS reports to SEC; failure to meet net capital = suspension.

---

## Civil and Criminal Liability
**§61 civil liability**: Seller liable to buyer for unregistered securities: consideration paid + interest − any income received. Buyer liable to seller for fraud in connection with purchase. Prescriptive period: 2 years from discovery; 5 years from violation (outer limit).

**§73 criminal liability**: Fine up to ₱5,000,000 AND/OR imprisonment up to 21 years. Corporate officers who participated in the violation are personally liable. Acquittal of corporation does not preclude conviction of officer (*People v. Nitafan* line of cases).

**SEC administrative penalties**: Cease and desist; revocation of registration; fine up to ₱1,000,000 per violation; trading suspension.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Securities Law
Not all securities require registration — §9 exempt securities and §10 exempt transactions are major exclusions. Howey test is for investment contracts specifically; do not mechanically apply without checking Philippine SC/SEC cases. Insider trading reaches tippees only when they knew information came from insider breach of duty. Mandatory tender offer threshold is set in SRC-IRR (35%) — subject to amendment. §10.1(a) private placement counts offerees (not purchasers). MNPI becomes public only after full and fair public disclosure plus market absorption time — not merely upon internal corporate knowledge. Civil prescriptive period is 2 years from discovery AND not more than 5 years from violation — both limits apply. Criminal and civil liability are independent; acquittal on criminal charges does not bar SEC administrative action or civil suit.

## Reporting and Disclosure Obligations — Detail
**Form 17-A (Annual Report)**: Management Discussion & Analysis; audited FS; information on business, properties, legal proceedings; directors and officers; executive compensation; certain relationships and related transactions; market price; dividends; 5-year financial highlights.

**Form 17-C (Current Report)**: Material events — change in fiscal year; change of directors/officers; change in auditor; pending legal proceedings; departure of senior officers; other events requiring immediate disclosure. Must be filed within the prescribed period (generally within 5 business days of occurrence).

**Material weakness in internal controls**: Auditor must report to audit committee; material weaknesses that are not remediated must be disclosed in annual report; implications for reliance on financial statements.

---

## Corporate Governance under SRC
**SRC §22**: Public companies must comply with SEC corporate governance rules. **SEC Memorandum Circular No. 19 (2016)**: Code of Corporate Governance for PLCs — mandatory for all SRC-covered issuers.

**Audit committee requirements**: At least 3 members; majority independent directors; chair must be independent director. Oversees integrity of financial reporting; engagement and independence of external auditor; internal audit function; risk oversight.

**Related party transactions**: PLCs must have RPT policy approved by board; material RPTs (exceeding thresholds) must be disclosed; evaluated by independent directors for arm's-length nature.

---

## Enforcement and Remedies
**SEC investigative powers**: Issue subpoenas; examine witnesses; inspect books; compel production of documents; order examination of any entity under its jurisdiction. Refusal to comply = contempt.

**Cease and desist orders (CDO)**: SEC may issue ex parte CDO to prevent imminent fraud or irreparable harm; effective immediately; subject to SEC hearing within 10 days; judicially reviewable via certiorari.

**Asset freeze orders**: SEC may apply with court for freeze of assets of respondents in fraud cases. Coordinated with AMLC for AML-related securities violations.

**Restitution fund**: SEC may order restitution to defrauded investors from proceeds of enforcement actions; separate from criminal penalties.

---

## Crowdfunding and Capital Markets Innovation
**SEC Crowdfunding Rules (MC No. 14, 2019)**: Issuer-type crowdfunding (equity/debt-based securities offerings through crowdfunding intermediaries); maximum ₱10M per offering; investors cap on individual investments based on income/net worth; intermediaries must register with SEC.

**SPAC (Special Purpose Acquisition Companies)**: SEC-approved framework for blank check companies that raise capital through IPO to acquire target business; governed by SEC SPAC Rules.

**Bonds and debt securities**: Corporate bonds require registration (unless exempt); rating by accredited credit rating agency for public offerings; trustees for bondholders required for listed bonds (Indenture).

**Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs, R.A. 9856)**: Listed companies owning income-generating real estate; required to distribute 90% of distributable income to shareholders; SEC-regulated; tax incentives under REIT Law.

**Alternative Trading Systems (ATS)**: Electronic platforms matching orders outside PSE; must register with SEC; subject to trading and reporting rules equivalent to exchange requirements.
