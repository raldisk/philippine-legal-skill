# Banking and Finance Law

## Primary Sources
**General Banking Law of 2000 (R.A. 8791)** — organization, operation, regulation of banks and quasi-banks. **New Central Bank Act (R.A. 7653 as amended by R.A. 11211)** — BSP establishment, powers, independence. **PDIC Charter (R.A. 3591 as amended)** — deposit insurance. Load `financial-regulation/anti-money-laundering.md` for AMLA/BSA obligations.

---

## Types of Banks
**Universal banks**: All commercial banking functions + investment house functions + equity investments. Highest capitalization.

**Commercial banks**: Accept deposits; make loans/discounts; issue letters of credit; deal in foreign exchange; discount commercial paper; other GBL-authorized services.

**Thrift banks**: Savings and mortgage banks, private development banks, stock savings and loan associations. Narrower scope, lower capitalization.

**Rural banks**: Serve rural communities; promote rural development.

**Cooperative banks**: Serve cooperative members; governed by GBL and Cooperative Code.

**Islamic banks (Al-Amanah, R.A. 6848)**: No interest; profit-sharing and lease arrangements per Islamic banking principles.

---

## BSP Regulatory Powers
**Monetary policy**: Money supply management; overnight lending/borrowing rates; foreign exchange reserves; inflation targeting.

**Supervisory powers**: Examine banks; issue Cease and Desist Orders (CDOs); impose sanctions; require corrective action plans.

**Conservatorship**: BSP places bank under conservator when illiquid or insolvent but not yet at receivership threshold. Conservator takes over management; bank continues operating.

**Receivership (§30 R.A. 7653)**: BSP may place bank under receivership if: (1) unable to pay liabilities as due in ordinary course; (2) insufficient realizable assets; (3) continuation involves probable losses to depositors/creditors; (4) willful violation of CDO; (5) unsafe/unsound practices. *BSP v. Banco Filipino*: BSP closure order is subject to judicial review for grave abuse of discretion.

**Liquidation**: PDIC acts as receiver and liquidator upon bank closure. PDIC insures deposits up to maximum amount per depositor per bank — verify current maximum against PDIC issuances.

---

## Lending and Credit
**Single Borrower's Limit (SBL, §35 GBL)**: No bank may extend loans, advances, and other credit accommodations to any single borrower exceeding 25% of net worth. DOSRI subject to stricter limits.

**DOSRI rules (§36 GBL)**: Directors, Officers, Stockholders, and Related Interests. DOSRI loans allowed if: borrower not past due; approved by majority of board excluding interested director; total DOSRI loans ≤ 100% of bank's equity. Violation is a criminal offense. *Soriano v. People*: DOSRI violation prosecutable under §83 R.A. 8791.

**Bank Secrecy (R.A. 1405)**: Deposits absolutely confidential. Exceptions: (1) written depositor consent; (2) impeachment cases; (3) court order in bribery/dereliction cases; (4) money is subject matter of litigation.

**Foreign Currency Deposit Secrecy (R.A. 6426)**: Stronger protection — no court-ordered disclosure without depositor consent. *Intengan v. Court of Appeals*: no exception for court orders even in criminal proceedings.

---

## Letters of Credit
**Three parties**: Applicant (buyer); issuing bank; beneficiary (seller).

**Independence principle**: L/C is independent of the underlying sale contract. Bank deals in documents, not goods. *Feati Bank & Trust Co. v. CA*: bank's obligation is to pay on conforming documents; it cannot go behind the documents.

**UCP 600**: International standard governing L/C transactions; incorporated by reference in most Philippine commercial bank L/Cs.

**Fraud exception**: Independence principle may be set aside only for clear, established fraud on the part of the beneficiary.

---

## Trust and Fiduciary Services
**Trust functions**: Separate BSP authorization required. Trust department segregated from regular banking operations. Fiduciary standard applies; trustee holds and administers property for beneficiary.

**Investment Management Accounts**: Managed on behalf of clients under agency agreement. NOT a deposit — not PDIC-insured.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Banking and Finance
Never state PDIC maximum coverage as a fixed peso amount — it is periodically amended; always direct to current PDIC issuances. R.A. 1405 (peso deposits) and R.A. 6426 (FCDU deposits) are separate statutes with different exceptions — R.A. 6426 has no court-ordered disclosure exception. Conservatorship ≠ receivership — conservatorship is rehabilitation; receivership → liquidation. SBL is 25% of net worth, not a fixed peso amount. DOSRI violation is both a regulatory and criminal violation. BSP closure orders are judicially reviewable for grave abuse; they are not unreviewable executive acts.

## Bank Capitalization and Organization
**Universal bank requirements**: Highest minimum capital (verify current BSP circular); must comply with BSP Manual of Regulations for Banks (MORB). Subject to equity investment limits under R.A. 8791 §24: up to 100% of voting stock of other financial intermediaries; equity investments in non-financial allied undertakings up to 35%; in non-allied undertakings up to 25%.

**Commercial bank requirements**: Lower minimum capital than universal banks; no investment house function; no equity investments in non-financial non-allied undertakings.

**Entry of foreign banks (R.A. 10641)**: Foreign banks may enter through: (1) acquisition of up to 100% of voting stock of existing domestic bank; (2) investment in up to 100% of voting stock of new banking subsidiary; (3) establishment of branches up to 100% foreign-owned. BSP may authorize up to 60% aggregate foreign ownership of total bank resources of the banking system.

---

## Reserve Requirements and Monetary Policy
**Reserve requirements**: BSP mandates banks maintain a percentage of deposits as reserves (cash in vault + deposits with BSP). Rate is adjusted via BSP Monetary Board Circular. Failure to meet reserves → penalty interest on deficiency.

**Overnight Reverse Repurchase (RRP) / Reverse Repo**: BSP's main policy rate; signals direction of monetary policy; affects interbank rates.

**Special Deposit Accounts (SDA)**: BSP facility accepting deposits from banks and trust entities to absorb excess liquidity; subject to adjustment by BSP.

---

## Lending Operations in Detail
**Loan classification**: BSP Circular 855 framework — pass; special mention; substandard; doubtful; loss. Banks must maintain General Loan Loss Provisions (GLLP) and specific provisions. Non-performing loans (NPL) — defined by BSP; affect capital ratios.

**Single Borrower's Limit (SBL §35 GBL)**: 25% of net worth per borrower; includes direct and indirect (guaranty-backed) exposure. Exceptions: fully secured government obligations; BSP-approved exceptions for specific development finance.

**DOSRI rules (§36 GBL)**: DOSRI = Directors, Officers, Stockholders (10%+), and Related Interests. DOSRI loans: (1) on market terms; (2) no past due; (3) approved by majority of board (interested director excluded); (4) reportable to BSP; (5) total DOSRI loans ≤ 15% of total loan portfolio. *Soriano v. People* (GR 159517): DOSRI violations are criminal offenses; prescriptive period runs from discovery.

---

## Deposits and Deposit Insurance
**Nature of bank deposit**: Creates debtor-creditor relationship (not trust). Bank may use deposits; depositor is unsecured creditor unless within PDIC coverage.

**PDIC (R.A. 3591 as amended by R.A. 10846)**: Insures deposits up to maximum coverage per depositor per bank — **verify current PDIC circular for exact peso amount** (amended periodically). Covers peso demand, savings, time deposits. Does NOT cover investment products, money market placements, repurchase agreements, trust accounts, or deposits made in violation of law.

**Exempt from PDIC insurance**: Deposits acquired through fraudulent means; deposits representing proceeds of money laundering; deposits classified as unsafe/unsound by BSP; deposits of directors/officers with DOSRI conflict.

## Bank Secrecy
**R.A. 1405 (peso deposits)**: Absolutely confidential. Exceptions — written depositor consent; orders of competent court in cases of bribery, dereliction of duty, or where the money is the subject matter of the litigation; impeachment cases; order of a competent court in cases falling under R.A. 3019 (Anti-Graft); upon inquiry by AMLC under R.A. 9160.

**R.A. 6426 (FCDU — Foreign Currency Deposits)**: Stronger protection; only exception is written depositor consent. No court-ordered disclosure. *Intengan v. CA* (GR 128996): no judicially compelled disclosure even in criminal proceedings; *Salvacion v. Central Bank* (GR 94723): courts may disregard bank secrecy when fundamental justice requires and victim is local resident (limited exception).

**AMLC inquiry (R.A. 9160 as amended)**: AMLC may inquire into or examine deposits without court order for covered/suspicious transactions; court order required for freeze orders and civil forfeiture.

## BSP Supervisory Tools
**Regular examination**: BSP examines banks at least once every year; results classified; deficiencies reported to board; corrective action plan required.

**Enforcement actions**: BSP may issue CDO; impose monetary penalties (per violation per day); suspend operations; restrict activities; disqualify directors/officers.

**Prompt Corrective Action (PCA)**: Mandatory BSP intervention when capital adequacy falls below minimum: capital restoration plan → restrictions → conservatorship → receivership.

**Conservatorship**: BSP appoints conservator to manage a bank that is or is likely to become unable to pay liabilities in ordinary course or has insufficient realizable assets, but where rehabilitation is still feasible. Conservatorship ≠ closure; bank continues operations.

**Receivership**: BSP places bank under receiver (PDIC) when: (1) unable to pay liabilities as they fall due; (2) insufficient realizable assets to meet liabilities; (3) continuation involves probable loss to depositors/creditors; (4) willful violation of CDO. *Rural Bank of Buhi v. CA* (GR 68507): BSP's authority to close bank is a valid exercise of police power; subject to judicial review only for grave abuse. *BSP v. Banco Filipino* (GR 200678): closure order reviewed for grave abuse; BSP must follow statutory grounds precisely.

---

## Electronic Banking and Digital Finance
**BSP Circular 649 (Electronic Money Issuers)**: EMI categories: banks and non-bank EMIs. E-money is not a deposit; not PDIC-insured. Must be redeemable at par.

**Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs, BSP Circular 1108)**: Entities facilitating virtual asset transactions must register with BSP; subject to AML/CFT obligations; consumer protection requirements.

**Open Finance (BSP Circular 1122)**: Framework for data portability, third-party access to financial data with customer consent; customer-centric.

---

## Quantum of Evidence in Banking Disputes
**Extraordinary diligence**: Banks, being impressed with public interest, are required to exercise a higher degree of diligence (extraordinary diligence) than ordinary private parties. *Westmont Bank v. Ong* (GR 132560): banks must ensure that transactions involving deposits are conducted with highest fidelity. Negligence of bank employees is the bank's negligence.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Banking and Finance
PDIC maximum coverage changes — never state a specific peso figure; direct to current PDIC circular. R.A. 1405 (peso) and R.A. 6426 (FCDU) have different exceptions — never conflate. Conservatorship ≠ receivership — conservatorship is rehabilitation; receivership leads to liquidation. SBL = 25% of net worth; verify exceptions from current MORB. DOSRI loans are regulatory AND criminal violations. Banks owe extraordinary diligence — higher than ordinary private parties. E-money is not a deposit and not PDIC-insured. BSP closure orders are reviewable for grave abuse of discretion only.

## Capital Adequacy Framework
**Basel III implementation (BSP Circular 781 series)**: Philippine banks subject to minimum Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR) of 10% (higher than Basel minimum of 8%). CAR = Total Qualifying Capital / Total Risk-Weighted Assets. Components: Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1); Additional Tier 1 (AT1); Tier 2 Capital.

**Capital conservation buffer**: 2.5% additional CET1 required on top of minimum; breach triggers restrictions on dividends and discretionary bonuses.

**Countercyclical capital buffer**: BSP may impose additional buffer (0–2.5%) during credit booms; released during stress periods.

**Leverage ratio**: Non-risk-weighted backstop; minimum 5% for domestic systemically important banks (D-SIBs).

## Anti-Money Laundering Obligations
**AMLA (R.A. 9160 as amended by R.A. 9194, 10167, 10365, 11521)**: Banks are covered institutions (CIs). Obligations: (1) Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and Know-Your-Customer (KYC); (2) reporting covered transactions (≥₱500,000 or foreign equivalent single-day aggregate); (3) reporting suspicious transactions regardless of amount; (4) record retention (5 years minimum).

**AMLC powers**: Without court order: examine deposits for covered/suspicious transactions. Via ex parte CA petition: freeze orders (Court of Appeals, up to 20 days initial; extended by CA on application); civil forfeiture (Regional Trial Court). For enumerated predicate offenses (kidnapping for ransom, drug trafficking, terrorism, etc.), bank-deposit inquiry needs no court order; for other predicate offenses, a CA order is required. Full AMLA doctrine in `financial-regulation/anti-money-laundering.md`.

**Safe harbor (§30 AMLA)**: Bank officers and employees acting in good faith to comply with AMLA reporting obligations are not liable for civil or criminal penalty. No liability for breach of bank secrecy if disclosure made under AMLA compliance.

---

## Trust and Fiduciary Business
**Trust license (BSP Manual Part V)**: Separate BSP authorization required for trust functions. Trust assets segregated from bank's own assets; trust assets not part of bank's estate upon insolvency. Trustee owes fiduciary duty: loyalty, prudence, impartiality.

**Prohibited self-dealing**: Trust department cannot engage in transactions with the bank's commercial department on behalf of trust clients without express client consent and disclosure.

**Unit Investment Trust Funds (UITFs)**: Pooled investment vehicle administered by trust entity; not a deposit; not PDIC-insured; disclosed to clients. Governed by BSP Circular 447; UITF value fluctuates with NAV.

---

## Quasi-Banks and Non-Bank Financial Institutions
**Quasi-banks (§4(b) R.A. 8791)**: Entities engaged in borrowing funds from the public through issuance of commercial papers; subject to BSP supervision. Finance companies (R.A. 8556): primarily make loans to consumers and business; supervised by SEC and BSP.

**Pawnshops**: Regulated by BSP under General Circular for Pawnshops. Must obtain BSP license; subject to KYC obligations.

**Money service businesses (MSBs)**: Remittance agents, money changers, virtual asset service providers — regulated by BSP for AML/CFT purposes under BSP Circular 471 series and updates.

---

## Consumer Protection in Banking
**BSP Financial Consumer Protection Framework (Circular 857)**: Banks must establish consumer protection mechanisms; handle complaints within prescribed periods; provide clear product disclosures; maintain consumer assistance desks.

**Truth in lending (R.A. 3765)**: Creditors must disclose finance charges, effective interest rate, and total amount to be paid before consummation of credit transaction. Non-disclosure entitles borrower to annul the credit transaction or recover finance charge overpayments.

**Usury Law suspension**: Monetary Board lifted usury ceilings (CB Circular 905, 1982); interest rates now market-determined. However, courts may still reduce excessive, unconscionable interest rates under Art. 1229 NCC and *Nacar v. Gallery Frames* (GR 189871): legal interest rate is 6% p.a. on monetary judgments.

**Financial Inclusion Framework (BSP Circular 940)**: BSP policy to extend financial services to unserved/underserved populations; basic deposit accounts; agent banking; microfinance.

**BSP Resolution Framework**: Banks declared insolvent by BSP may be subject to Purchase and Assumption (P&A) — good assets and assumed liabilities transferred to another bank — or bridge bank mechanism; speeds resolution; minimizes disruption to depositors. PDIC facilitates P&A as preferred resolution tool over straight liquidation.

**Mortgage and collateral documentation**: Real estate mortgage (REM) must be registered with Registry of Deeds to bind third parties; chattel mortgage registered with Register of Deeds for personal property or LTO for vehicles; pledge requires delivery of possession. Extrajudicial foreclosure under Act 3135: notice, publication, auction, sheriff's certificate; redemption period 1 year (banks under R.A. 8791: 3 months or before registration of sale, whichever earlier).

**Securitization (R.A. 9267)**: Banks may sell receivables to Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV); SPV issues asset-backed securities (ABS); removes assets from bank balance sheet; derecognition subject to BSP rules. SPV Act extended by subsequent laws for NPL resolution.

**Anti-Dummy Law (C.A. 108)**: Prohibits Filipinos from allowing foreign nationals to use their name or citizenship to circumvent nationality restrictions on ownership of banks or financial institutions. Violations: criminal liability for both dummy and foreign national.
**Rural Banking Act (R.A. 7353)**: Rural banks serve agricultural and rural communities; may accept savings/time deposits; make loans for agricultural and rural purposes; supervised by BSP; PDIC-insured deposits. Microfinance products and agri-loans incentivized under BSP regulations.
