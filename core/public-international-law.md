# Public International Law Module

## Incorporation Doctrine vs. Transformation Doctrine

**Article II, §2, 1987 Constitution**: "The Philippines renounces war as an instrument of national policy, adopts the generally accepted principles of international law as part of the law of the land and adheres to the policy of peace, equality, justice, freedom, cooperation, and amity with all nations."

**Incorporation doctrine**: Generally accepted principles of international law (customary international law and general principles) are automatically part of Philippine law without need for legislative implementation. The Philippines follows incorporation.

**Transformation doctrine**: International law becomes domestic law only when enacted into domestic legislation. The Philippines does not follow pure transformation, but in practice, treaty obligations often require implementing legislation for domestic enforcement.

**Treaties**: Ratified treaties are part of the law of the land. They are superior to statutes in the international sphere, but domestically the Philippines applies the later-in-time rule for conflicts between treaties and statutes (*Ichong v. Hernandez*, 101 Phil. 1155, 1957: Retail Trade Nationalization Law upheld even though it conflicted with treaty obligations — statute prevailed domestically).

## Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties

Key principles applicable in Philippine treaty analysis:

**Pacta sunt servanda** (Article 26): Every treaty in force is binding on parties and must be performed in good faith.

**Good faith interpretation** (Article 31): A treaty shall be interpreted in good faith in accordance with ordinary meaning of terms in their context and in the light of its object and purpose.

**Subsequent practice** (Article 31(3)(b)): Subsequent practice in application of the treaty which establishes the parties' agreement regarding its interpretation counts as an interpretive tool.

**Reservations**: A reservation modifies treaty obligations for the reserving state. The Philippines has entered reservations to certain treaties. Always verify the specific reservation text.

## UNCLOS — West Philippine Sea Framework

**Philippines' accession**: The Philippines ratified UNCLOS on May 8, 1984. UNCLOS entered into force November 16, 1994.

**Relevant UNCLOS zones**:
- **Territorial sea**: 12 nautical miles from baselines; full sovereignty
- **Contiguous zone**: 24 nm; enforcement jurisdiction for customs, fiscal, immigration, sanitary
- **Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ)**: 200 nm from baselines; sovereign rights over resources; jurisdiction over artificial islands, marine research, marine environment protection
- **Continental shelf**: Rights over seabed and subsoil; may extend beyond 200 nm under Article 76

**Baseline law**: Republic Act 9522 (2009) defined Philippine archipelagic baselines, conforming the country's baseline claims to UNCLOS. However, the Kalayaan Island Group (KIG) and Scarborough Shoal are not included as part of the Philippine archipelago — they are designated as "Regime of Islands" under UNCLOS Article 121.

## South China Sea Arbitration (Philippines v. China)

**Formal citation**: *The Republic of Philippines v. The People's Republic of China* (PCA Case No. 2013-19, Award of July 12, 2016, Permanent Court of Arbitration)

**Jurisdiction**: UNCLOS Annex VII tribunal. China declined to participate but the tribunal found jurisdiction.

**Key holdings** (summarized — verify against original text):
- China's "nine-dash line" claim to historic rights over the South China Sea has no basis in UNCLOS to the extent it exceeds UNCLOS entitlements
- Mischief Reef, McKennan Reef, Gaven Reef (South), Subi Reef are low-tide elevations generating no maritime zones of their own
- Scarborough Shoal is a rock under Article 121(3) entitled to a 12nm territorial sea but not an EEZ or continental shelf
- China's interference with Philippine fishing at Scarborough Shoal violated the Philippines' sovereign rights
- China's construction activities at several features caused serious harm to the coral reef ecosystem

**China's position**: China has rejected the award as null and void, holding it has no binding force. China does not recognize the tribunal's jurisdiction.

**Philippine enforcement status**: The Philippines has taken varying positions across administrations on how actively to invoke the award in bilateral diplomacy. The award remains a binding international legal determination regardless of China's non-compliance.

**Legal weight for Philippine courts**: As a treaty instrument and binding arbitral award under UNCLOS, the South China Sea Award has the status of an international legal determination. Philippine courts applying UNCLOS would give it weight. However, enforcement against China domestically is politically and legally complex.

## Mutual Defense Treaties and Military Agreements

**RP-US Mutual Defense Treaty** (1951): Each party commits to act to meet common dangers in the Pacific area if either is attacked. *Bayan v. Zamora* (2000): VFA held constitutional as an executive agreement on foreign military forces in the Philippines.

**Visiting Forces Agreement (VFA)**: *Bayan v. Zamora* (2000): Upheld as a treaty requiring Senate concurrence; Senate's concurrence in 1999 was valid.

**EDCA (Enhanced Defense Cooperation Agreement)**: *Saguisag v. Ochoa* (2016): EDCA is an executive agreement implementing the VFA and MDT; no separate Senate concurrence required. Allows US access to "Agreed Locations" within Philippine military bases.

## State Responsibility

Under customary international law (codified in the ILC Articles on Responsibility of States for Internationally Wrongful Acts):

An internationally wrongful act consists of (a) conduct attributable to the State and (b) a breach of an international obligation of the State.

**Attribution**: Conduct of state organs, persons exercising governmental authority, persons acting on instructions or under direction and control of the State.

**Circumstances precluding wrongfulness**: Consent, self-defense, countermeasures, force majeure, distress, necessity.

**Relevance to Philippine context**: Attribution of drug war killings to the Philippine state as state-sponsored acts for ICC purposes requires demonstrating the conduct was attributable to state organs or persons acting under state direction.

## Diplomatic Law

**Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations** (1961): Philippines is a party. Diplomatic agents enjoy immunity from criminal jurisdiction of the receiving state. Inviolability of mission premises.

**Vienna Convention on Consular Relations** (1963): Philippines is a party. Consular officers have functional immunity (acts performed in exercise of consular functions).

**Sovereign immunity**: A foreign state and its property are immune from the jurisdiction of another state's courts, subject to the restrictive theory exceptions for commercial activity (*Minucher v. CA* and related cases: scope of diplomatic immunity analysis).
## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Public International Law

Never state that China's rejection of the South China Sea Arbitration Award nullifies its legal effect — China's non-participation and non-compliance are political facts; the Award remains a binding international legal determination under UNCLOS Annex VII for the Philippines and is the baseline for Philippine legal claims in the WPS. Never apply pure transformation doctrine to Philippine treaty analysis — the Philippines follows incorporation for customary international law (Art. II, §2) but treaties may still require implementing legislation for domestic enforcement; the two doctrines have distinct scopes. Never state that the VFA required no Senate concurrence — *Bayan v. Zamora* (2000) held the VFA is a treaty requiring Senate concurrence, which was given in 1999. Never state that EDCA required Senate concurrence — *Saguisag v. Ochoa* (2016) held EDCA is an executive agreement implementing existing treaties and does not require separate Senate concurrence. UNCLOS jurisdictional zone measurements depend on properly established baselines under R.A. 9522 — never apply UNCLOS distances without identifying the correct baseline.
