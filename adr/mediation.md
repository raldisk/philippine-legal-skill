# Mediation

## Primary Sources
**Alternative Dispute Resolution Act of 2004 (R.A. 9285) Chapter 3** — statutory framework for mediation. **Special ADR Rules (A.M. No. 07-11-08-SC, Rule 11)** — court-annexed mediation procedures. **DOJ-OAC (Office for Alternative Dispute Resolution)** — executive agency for ADR promotion. **Philippine Mediation Center (PMC)** — administered through the Office of the Court Administrator (OCA/SC); implements court-connected mediation programs.

---

## Nature

Mediation is a voluntary process where a **neutral third party (mediator)** facilitates communication between disputing parties to assist them in reaching a mutually acceptable settlement. Distinguished from arbitration: the mediator has no decision-making authority — any settlement reached is by the parties themselves, not imposed by the mediator.

---

## Types of Mediation in the Philippines

**Voluntary mediation (private)**: Parties agree to mediate outside of court. Governed by R.A. 9285 Chapter 3. Any person may serve as mediator if agreed upon by parties. No court involvement required unless settlement needs judicial confirmation.

**Court-Annexed Mediation (CAM)**: Conducted under SC supervision through the PMC. Parties in civil cases before the RTC, MTC, and other first-level courts are **required** to undergo CAM before trial. The PMC accredits mediators. CAM is mandatory in: ordinary civil actions; civil cases cognizable by first-level courts; and cases not exempt under the Rules.

**Judicial Dispute Resolution (JDR)**: After failed CAM, in certain cases the judge (a different judge from the one who will try the case) acts as conciliator/early neutral evaluator. Applied in cases still within the court's jurisdiction and at a stage where settlement remains possible. Participation in JDR is mandatory; non-appearance without valid cause is sanctioned.

**Online Mediation**: Permitted under Supreme Court rules for appropriate cases; particularly expanded during post-pandemic court operations.

---

## Cases Covered by Court-Annexed Mediation

CAM applies to all civil cases **except**: (1) habeas corpus; (2) election cases; (3) cases not susceptible to compromise under Art. 2035 NCC (civil status, validity of marriage, grounds for legal separation, future legitime, criminal liability); (4) cases where the government or a government instrumentality is a party, unless the OSG or government counsel agrees to participate.

---

## Mediator Standards and Confidentiality (R.A. 9285 §9–16)

**Neutrality**: Mediator must be impartial and disclose any conflict of interest. If circumstances create justifiable doubt, mediator must withdraw or parties may object.

**Confidentiality**: All information disclosed in mediation proceedings is **confidential**. A mediator may not be compelled to testify about what occurred during mediation in any subsequent legal or arbitral proceeding. Documents prepared for or during mediation are protected from disclosure. Exceptions: evidence of abuse, neglect, or criminal conduct; waiver by all parties; threat to public safety.

**Non-binding on judges**: A JDR judge who facilitated settlement discussions may not preside over the trial if mediation fails — the case is re-raffled or assigned to another judge to preserve impartiality.

---

## Mediation Settlement Agreement

**Binding effect**: A settlement agreement reached in mediation is binding on the parties as a contract. To acquire the enforcement force of a final judgment, it must be approved by the court (if court-annexed) or confirmed in a **compromise judgment** under Rule 18 of the Rules of Court.

**Effect of approved settlement**: Has the force and effect of a final judgment; executory; not appealable as to the merits of the compromise.

**Effect of failed mediation**: Mediation history (offers, concessions, admissions) is **inadmissible** in subsequent trial on the merits. Confidentiality rule prevents use of mediation disclosures as evidence.

---

## Mediator's Role vs. Arbitrator's Role

| Feature | Mediation | Arbitration |
|---|---|---|
| Decision-maker | Parties | Arbitrator(s) |
| Outcome | Voluntary settlement or no agreement | Binding award |
| Mediator authority | Facilitate; no power to decide | Full adjudicatory authority |
| Appealability | Settlement = contract; court approval = judgment | Award: limited vacatur grounds |
| Confidentiality | Strong statutory protection | Limited but present |

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Mediation
Court-Annexed Mediation is mandatory before trial in civil cases — it is not optional for the parties. The mediator has NO authority to impose a decision; any outcome must be the parties' own agreement. Mediation confidentiality is statutory and strong — communications during mediation are protected from use in subsequent court proceedings. A JDR judge who participated in conciliation efforts must NOT try the case on the merits if mediation/JDR fails — a different judge must be assigned. Settlement agreements from mediation are binding contracts; they only acquire force of final judgment upon court approval or compromise judgment. Cases involving civil status, validity of marriage, and future legitimes cannot be compromised and are excluded from CAM. Never state that a mediator's recommendation is binding — it is not.
