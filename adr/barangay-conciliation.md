# Barangay Conciliation (Katarungang Pambarangay)

## Primary Sources
**Local Government Code of 1991 (R.A. 7160) Chapter 7 (Secs. 399–422)** — Katarungang Pambarangay (KP); supersedes PD 1508 (Barangay Justice System). **Lupong Tagapamayapa Implementing Rules** — DILG issuances on composition and procedure.

---

## Purpose and Nature

The Katarungang Pambarangay is a **barangay-level conciliation system** designed to decongest courts by requiring disputing parties to attempt amicable settlement before resorting to litigation. It is a **condition precedent to court action** for covered disputes. Non-compliance may result in dismissal of the court action.

---

## Lupon Tagapamayapa

**Composition (§399)**: Barangay chairman (Punong Barangay) + 10 to 20 members chosen from among barangay residents. The Punong Barangay chairs the Lupon.

**Pangkat ng Tagapagkasundo**: A panel of three members drawn from the Lupon, constituted for each specific dispute. The Pangkat conducts the conciliation proceedings.

**Qualifications of members (§399)**: Residents of the barangay; must be willing to serve; no conviction for crime involving moral turpitude; must be of good moral character and integrity; ability to attend meetings.

---

## Covered Disputes — When KP is Required (§408)

KP conciliation is **mandatory before filing a court action** when ALL of the following conditions are met:
1. The **dispute is between natural persons** (juridical entities are generally not parties before the Lupon).
2. The parties **actually reside in the same city or municipality**.
3. The dispute does not fall within any exception below.

Note: Where parties reside in the **same barangay**, the dispute must first be filed with that barangay's Lupon.

---

## Exceptions — When KP Does NOT Apply (§408)

KP conciliation does NOT apply where:
- One party is the **government** or a government instrumentality/subdivision.
- One party is not a **natural person** (corporations, cooperatives, juridical entities).
- The dispute involves **real property** located in different barangays, unless parties reside in the same barangay.
- The dispute involves **criminal offenses** with prescribed imprisonment exceeding **one year** or a fine exceeding **five thousand pesos (₱5,000)**.
- The offense is charged under **special laws** where pre-trial procedure is different from ordinary criminal cases (*including drug offenses, anti-graft*).
- Urgent legal relief is being sought, including provisional remedies (injunction, TRO, attachment).
- The parties reside in **different municipalities** unless they agree to participate in KP conciliation voluntarily.
- Actions **involving labor disputes** covered by the Labor Code.
- Cases under **VAWC (RA 9262)**, anti-trafficking, and other protective orders where direct conciliation may endanger victims.

---

## Procedure (§§410–415)

**Filing**: Written or oral complaint to Punong Barangay. Punong Barangay summons the respondent and attempts conciliation personally.

**Pangkat constitution**: If Punong Barangay's mediation fails, Pangkat of three is constituted within 3 days; parties must appear before Pangkat within 15 days from constitution.

**Period for settlement**: Lupon/Pangkat has **30 days** from first meeting to reach settlement; may be extended by agreement of the parties.

**Repudiation**: Settlement may be repudiated within 10 days from execution; if repudiated, parties obtain certification to file in court.

**Execution of Lupon award**: A settlement or an arbitration award under KP may be executed by the Lupon within **6 months** from date of settlement/award. After 6 months, execution requires filing with MTC/MCTC.

---

## Certificate to File Action (§412)

A party may not go to court on a covered dispute **without first obtaining a Certificate to File Action** from the Punong Barangay or Pangkat, certifying that: (1) no settlement was reached; or (2) settlement was repudiated; or (3) one party refused to comply with terms.

**Effect of non-compliance**: Filing a court action without the certificate is a ground for **dismissal** of the complaint for failure to comply with a condition precedent. This is NOT jurisdictional in the strict sense — courts have held it is a procedural requirement that may be waived if not raised by the defendant (*Royales v. IAC*; *Morata v. Go*).

---

## Settlement and Arbitration Before the Lupon

**Compromise/Settlement**: Parties may enter a written amicable settlement before the Lupon. Has force and effect of a final judgment; enforceable by writ of execution issued by Lupon (within 6 months) or MTC/MCTC (after 6 months).

**Pangkat arbitration**: Parties may consent to submit their dispute to the Pangkat for binding arbitration — the Pangkat then renders a decision. Award is final and unappealable.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Barangay Conciliation
Katarungang Pambarangay applies only to disputes between **natural persons** who reside in the **same city or municipality** — juridical entities (corporations, cooperatives) are generally not proper parties. Criminal cases with imprisonment exceeding one year or fine exceeding ₱5,000 are EXEMPT from KP — do not apply KP to serious crimes. Non-compliance with KP is a ground for dismissal but is NOT a jurisdictional defect — it is waived if the defendant fails to raise it in a motion to dismiss or answer. A settlement before the Lupon/Pangkat has the force of final judgment and may be enforced by writ of execution; it is NOT merely a contract requiring new court action for enforcement (though enforcement through the MTC is required after 6 months). Cases under VAWC, anti-trafficking, and similar protective-order laws are excluded from mandatory KP — never direct a victim of domestic abuse to first undergo barangay conciliation. Repudiation of a settlement must be done within 10 days of execution.
