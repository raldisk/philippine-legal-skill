# Arbitration

## Primary Sources
**Alternative Dispute Resolution Act of 2004 (R.A. 9285)** — governing statute for domestic and international commercial arbitration. **Arbitration Law (R.A. 876)** — applies to domestic arbitration proceedings unless parties choose R.A. 9285 Model Law track. **Special ADR Rules (A.M. No. 07-11-08-SC, 2009)** — SC rules governing court assistance and recognition of arbitral proceedings and awards. **UNCITRAL Model Law on International Commercial Arbitration (1985, Philippines adopted)** — governs international commercial arbitration under R.A. 9285. **New York Convention (1958, Philippines a signatory)** — foreign arbitral award enforcement. **Executive Order No. 1008 (1985)** — establishes CIAC for construction disputes.

---

## Nature and Scope

**Defined**: Arbitration is a voluntary dispute resolution process where parties agree to submit their dispute to one or more arbitrators who render a final and binding decision (award). It is consensual, private, and an alternative to litigation.

**Types under Philippine law**:
- **Domestic arbitration**: Parties and dispute are domestic; governed by R.A. 876 or R.A. 9285 Chapter 4. R.A. 876 applies unless parties opt for the Model Law track.
- **International commercial arbitration**: (1) parties from different states; or (2) place of business in different states; or (3) subject matter in a different state; or (4) agreement incorporates international commercial arbitration. Governed by UNCITRAL Model Law per R.A. 9285 Chapter 5.
- **Statutory/compulsory arbitration**: CIAC — construction disputes; NLRC voluntary arbitration — CBA and unresolved labor disputes.

---

## Arbitration Agreement

**Form**: Must be in writing. An arbitration clause within a contract or a separate submission agreement both qualify. An arbitration clause in a contract is **separable** from the main contract — the clause survives even if the contract is void or rescinded (*Transfield Philippines v. Luzon Hydro Corp.*, GR 146717).

**Scope**: The parties define the scope. Disputes not covered by the agreement are not arbitrable.

**Non-arbitrable matters** (R.A. 9285 §6): Labor disputes covered by Labor Code; civil status; validity of marriage; grounds for legal separation; jurisdiction of courts; future legitimes; criminal liability; interests of third parties not party to the agreement.

---

## Kompetenz-Kompetenz (R.A. 9285 §24)

The arbitral tribunal has authority to determine its own jurisdiction, including objections to the validity or existence of the arbitration agreement. A court seized of a matter in breach of an arbitration agreement must refer the parties to arbitration **on the request of a party**, not sua sponte. The court shall not refuse referral except where the agreement is null, void, inoperative, or incapable of performance.

---

## CIAC — Construction Industry Arbitration Commission

**Jurisdiction (EO 1008, §4)**: Exclusive and original jurisdiction over disputes arising from or connected with contracts entered into by parties involved in construction in the Philippines — including those between developers and contractors, owners and contractors, and contractors and subcontractors.

**Trigger**: Dispute covered by a CIAC arbitration clause → CIAC jurisdiction is mandatory; parties cannot opt out once a construction contract CIAC clause exists (*IBEX International v. GSIS*, GR 162191).

**Award**: Final and executory; CIAC rules allow limited judicial review only on jurisdictional errors or patent illegality.

---

## Conduct of Arbitration

**Arbitrators**: Parties choose; absent agreement — 1 arbitrator (domestic) or 3-arbitrator panel per applicable rules. Requirements: legal age, civil capacity, no interest in the outcome.

**Procedure**: Arbitrators determine procedural rules subject to the parties' agreement and applicable statutes. Both parties must be given equal opportunity to present evidence and argument.

**Award**: In writing; signed by majority; must state reasons unless parties agree otherwise (international); or the award is on agreed terms. Final and binding on the parties.

---

## Grounds for Vacating or Setting Aside an Arbitral Award

**Domestic (R.A. 876 §24)**: (1) Award procured by corruption, fraud, or undue means; (2) evident partiality or corruption in arbitrators; (3) arbitrators refused to postpone hearing upon sufficient cause shown, refused to hear pertinent evidence, or otherwise misbehaved; (4) arbitrators exceeded their powers.

**International — Model Law grounds (Art. 34)**: (1) incapacity of a party or invalidity of agreement; (2) no proper notice to party; (3) award deals with matters beyond submission; (4) arbitral tribunal composition violated agreement; (5) subject matter not arbitrable under Philippine law; (6) award conflicts with public policy.

Courts do NOT review the merits of the award. Judicial review is limited to the enumerated grounds.

---

## Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards

**New York Convention (Art. V)**: Philippine courts must enforce a foreign arbitral award unless: (1) party incapacity or agreement invalidity; (2) no notice of proceedings; (3) award beyond submission; (4) improper tribunal composition or procedure; (5) award not yet binding or suspended; (6) subject matter not arbitrable under Philippine law; (7) enforcement contrary to Philippine public policy.

**Procedure**: Petition to RTC within 30 days from receipt of award; court may refuse only on Art. V grounds; appeal to CA.

---

## Court Assistance Under Special ADR Rules (A.M. No. 07-11-08-SC)

**RTC may assist arbitration in**: Appointment of arbitrators; challenge of arbitrators; termination of mandate; interim measures of protection; preliminary injunctions; recognition and enforcement of awards; setting aside domestic awards; enforcement of foreign awards.

**Confidentiality**: Arbitration proceedings are confidential. Documents submitted and deliberations of tribunal are not subject to disclosure without consent of both parties or court order in exceptional cases.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Arbitration
An arbitration agreement is separable from the main contract — invalidity of the main contract does not automatically render the arbitration clause invalid. Kompetenz-kompetenz means the arbitral tribunal decides its own jurisdiction first; courts should refer to arbitration if asked and not proceed to rule on jurisdiction until after the arbitral tribunal has had the opportunity to do so. Courts do NOT review the merits of arbitral awards — only the enumerated vacatur grounds. CIAC jurisdiction over construction disputes is mandatory where an arbitration clause exists; parties cannot by subsequent agreement remove disputes from CIAC by choosing ordinary courts. The New York Convention applies to enforcement of foreign awards; domestic awards are governed by R.A. 876/R.A. 9285 and the Special ADR Rules. Never state that labor disputes under the Labor Code are generally arbitrable through commercial arbitration — the Labor Code's exclusive jurisdiction rules apply.
