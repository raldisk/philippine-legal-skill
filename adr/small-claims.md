# Small Claims

## Primary Sources
**Rule of Procedure for Small Claims Cases (A.M. No. 08-8-7-SC)** — SC promulgated 2008, subsequently amended (2010, 2016, 2022). **Revised Rules of Court, Rule 140** — on sanctions. The small claims rules are **self-contained and apply exclusively** to covered actions in first-level courts.

**Monetary threshold**: ₱1,000,000 and below (as of the 2022 amendment). Verify the current applicable threshold in the most recent SC amendment of A.M. No. 08-8-7-SC, as this has been raised progressively.

---

## Coverage and Purpose

Small claims procedure provides a **speedy, inexpensive, and simplified process** for resolving money claims of limited value without full-scale litigation. The objective is to allow litigants — particularly individuals without counsel — to assert small monetary claims without the complexity and cost of ordinary civil procedure.

---

## Covered Actions (§4)

Small claims procedure applies to actions **purely for payment of money** where the claim or relief sought is exclusively monetary. Covered claims include:

- **Contracts of loan or credit**: recovery of money based on written instruments of indebtedness (promissory notes, checks, credit card agreements).
- **Lease of real or personal property**: recovery of unpaid rentals, deposits, or damages arising from lease.
- **Services**: recovery of money for services rendered under a contract.
- **Sale of personal property**: claims for the purchase price.
- **Mortgage**: money claims secured by personal property where plaintiff seeks payment only (not foreclosure).
- **Damages**: liquidated or other damages arising from any of the foregoing, if sought alongside or instead of the principal claim.

---

## Excluded Actions (§4)

Small claims does NOT cover:
- Claims for real property (title, ownership, possession — not small claims).
- Actions seeking any relief other than payment of money (injunction, reconveyance, declaration, etc.).
- Claims against the government or government instrumentalities (jurisdictional bar).
- Claims where the amount exceeds the monetary threshold.
- Actions requiring probate proceedings or settlement of estate.

---

## Parties and Representation (§5–§11)

**No lawyers in court**: Parties to small claims cases must appear **in person** — lawyers may NOT appear on behalf of a party except: (1) the lawyer is himself/herself the plaintiff or defendant; (2) the lawyer is representing a corporation/juridical entity as its duly authorized officer (not as counsel in the professional sense). This rule prevents the adversarial advantage of legal representation and ensures parties are on equal footing.

**Authorized representative**: A corporation or juridical entity must send a representative who is its officer or duly authorized agent — not outside counsel.

---

## Procedure (§§12–24)

**Filing**: Small Claims Form (prescribed by SC) filed with the appropriate first-level court (MTC, MeTC, MCTC, MTCC). Filing fee applicable.

**Summons**: Court issues summons to defendant with a copy of the statement of claim and a response form.

**Response**: Defendant files response within 10 days from service. Counterclaims must be filed with the response.

**Hearing**: Set not more than **30 days** from filing of response (or expiration of period). Hearing is **informal and non-adversarial** — judge facilitates discussion and settlement. Rules of evidence apply liberally. Judge may informally examine witnesses.

**Settlement at hearing**: If parties settle, settlement is reduced to writing, signed by parties, and approved by court — constitutes final and executory judgment.

**Decision**: If no settlement, **decision is rendered on the same day** of hearing. Decision must be in writing but need not contain findings of fact and conclusions of law (unlike ordinary civil cases).

---

## Finality and Non-Appealability (§23)

Small claims decisions are **final, executory, and unappealable**. No appeal or motion for reconsideration is allowed on the merits. The **only remedy** against a small claims judgment is a **petition for certiorari** under Rule 65 — and only on the ground of **lack or excess of jurisdiction**, not on errors of law or fact. The prohibition on appeal is constitutional — the SC has authority under Art. VIII §5 to promulgate rules of procedure.

---

## Execution (§24)

Execution of the small claims judgment follows the ordinary rules on execution (Rule 39, Rules of Court) upon finality. If defendant fails to pay, plaintiff may seek issuance of a writ of execution from the same court.

---

## Key Distinctions from Ordinary Civil Procedure

| Feature | Small Claims | Ordinary Civil Action |
|---|---|---|
| Threshold | ≤₱1,000,000 (current) | Any amount |
| Lawyers | Not allowed (except as party) | Allowed |
| Hearing date | Within 30 days of response | Per case management |
| Decision | Same day of hearing | Later; with full findings |
| Appealability | NOT appealable on merits | Full appeal available |
| Certiorari | Only for jurisdiction | Available |
| Rules of evidence | Applied liberally | Strictly applied |

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Small Claims
The small claims monetary threshold has been progressively raised by SC amendment — **always verify the current amount** in the most recent version of A.M. No. 08-8-7-SC rather than citing a prior threshold as current. Lawyers may NOT appear for parties in small claims proceedings as counsel — a lawyer who is the party may appear in his/her personal capacity only. Small claims decisions are FINAL and NOT appealable on the merits; the only challenge is certiorari for jurisdictional errors. The certiorari remedy is extremely narrow — errors of judgment (wrong computation, wrong application of law on the merits) are NOT correctible by certiorari; only lack or excess of jurisdiction. Small claims covers only money claims — never apply it to actions for reconveyance, ejectment, or injunctions. Government entities are not proper defendants in small claims proceedings.
