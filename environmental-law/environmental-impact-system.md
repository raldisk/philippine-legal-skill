# Environmental Impact Assessment System (P.D. 1586)

## Primary Sources
**Presidential Decree 1586 (1978)** — EIS System. **DENR DAO 2003-30 (as amended by DAO 2017-15)** — implementing rules. **DENR-EMB (Environmental Management Bureau)** — administers EIS System; issues Environmental Compliance Certificates (ECC).

---

## Coverage
Projects requiring an ECC must be covered by DENR DAO — classified as **Environmentally Critical Projects (ECPs)** or projects in **Environmentally Critical Areas (ECAs)**. ECPs include: heavy industries; resource extractive industries; infrastructure projects; golf courses; reclamation projects. ECAs: areas declared environmentally critical by Presidential Proclamation.

Projects that are neither ECP nor in an ECA may file a Project Description (PD) for a Certificate of Non-Coverage (CNC).

---

## EIS Process
**Preparatory phase**: Scoping — identification of significant impacts; determination of EIS requirements.

**EIS preparation**: Proponent prepares Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) covering: project description; environmental setting; predicted impacts; mitigation measures; Environmental Management Plan (EMP); Social Development Program.

**Public participation**: Mandatory public scoping and public hearings (for ECPs and ECA projects with significant impacts).

**EMB review**: Technical review; site inspection; ECC issuance or denial.

**Environmental Compliance Certificate (ECC)**: Issued by DENR-EMB; authorizes the project subject to conditions. **Required before construction or operation begins.**

---

## Requirements During Operations
**Annual ECC compliance monitoring**: Proponent must submit Compliance Monitoring Reports (CMR).

**Environmental Guarantee Fund (EGF)**: Required for high-risk projects; covers potential damage.

**Multi-Partite Monitoring Team (MMT)**: Community-based monitoring for ECC compliance.

---

## Penalties
**Without ECC**: Criminal liability; fines; closure of project. *Republic v. City of Davao* (verify GR): projects operating without ECC subject to closure.

**ECC violation**: Administrative fines; suspension/revocation of ECC.

---

## Strategic Environmental Assessment (SEA)
Required for policies, plans, and programs (PPPs) that may significantly affect the environment — the policy-level analog to project-level EIS. DENR and NEDA coordinate.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — EIS System
The ECC is required **before** construction or operation begins — not after. Not all projects require an ECC — only ECPs and projects in ECAs; other projects may get a Certificate of Non-Coverage (CNC). The distinction between ECP and ECA classification determines the applicable review track. Operating without an ECC is a criminal violation — never advise that a proponent may begin construction while awaiting ECC issuance. Public hearings are mandatory for ECPs and significant ECA projects — but the scoping stage determines the required level of public participation.
---

## ECC Process — Technical Details

**Scoping**: First step — determines the significant environmental issues that the EIS must address. Mandatory for ECPs. Conducted by proponent with EMB; Scoping Summary Report (SSR) prepared. Defines Terms of Reference (TOR) for EIS preparation.

**Environmental Management Plan (EMP)**: Core component of the EIS; describes mitigation measures for each identified impact; includes monitoring program, implementation schedule, and budget. EMP becomes a condition of the ECC.

**EIS consultant requirements**: EIS preparers must be accredited by DENR-EMB; multi-disciplinary team required for major ECPs; team composition must include environment, health, social, engineering, and legal expertise.

**Community and public participation**: For major ECPs — two-stage public participation: (1) information and communication campaign; (2) formal public hearing. Minutes of public hearings are part of the EIS.

**Processing period**: EMB must decide within prescribed periods after complete EIS submission — historically 120 working days for ECPs in the national scope; regional scope follows regional EMB processing times.

---

## Environmental Compliance Certificate (ECC) — Conditions

**Standard conditions**: Compliance with EMP; submission of Compliance Monitoring Reports (CMR) semi-annually; maintenance of Environmental Guarantee Fund (EGF); Multi-Partite Monitoring Team (MMT) formation.

**Special conditions**: Project-specific requirements based on identified significant impacts — e.g., revegetation, species relocation, construction of settling ponds, monitoring wells, noise barriers.

**Amendment of ECC**: Material changes in project design, scope, or location require ECC amendment (new EIS may be required); minor changes — proponent notifies EMB and submits supplemental EIS.

**Suspension/cancellation of ECC**: EMB may suspend or cancel ECC for: non-compliance with conditions; misrepresentation in the EIS; violation of environmental laws; threat to public health and safety.

---

## Environmental Guarantee Fund (EGF)

**Purpose**: Funds cleanup, rehabilitation, and compensation for environmental damage caused by the project. Required for high-risk projects (e.g., mining, chemical facilities, large-scale infrastructure).

**Forms**: Cash deposit; surety bond; trust fund; combination. Amount based on estimated environmental liability.

**Release of EGF**: Upon satisfactory completion of project decommissioning and rehabilitation; requires EMB verification.

---

## Multi-Partite Monitoring Team (MMT)

**Composition**: Proponent; affected communities; LGU representative; DENR-EMB; relevant government agencies; NGOs. Meets regularly to monitor ECC compliance and MMT decisions are reported to EMB.

**Authority**: MMT may recommend actions to EMB for non-compliance; cannot issue orders independently but is an early warning system.

---

## ECC in the Context of Climate Change

**Climate change vulnerability assessment**: DENR DAO 2017-15 requires ECPs in climate-sensitive areas (coastal, watershed) to include a Climate Change Risk Assessment in the EIS. Climate adaptation measures must be incorporated into the EMP.

**Green building and energy efficiency**: Projects that include green building features (BERDE certification) or renewable energy may receive streamlined review for the environmental aspects covered by those certifications.

---

## Key Cases — EIS System

**Republic v. City of Davao** (GR 148622, 2003): City of Davao's anti-discrimination ordinance conflicted with national policy in ECC issuance; national standards under PD 1586 prevail over local ordinances; uniformity of EIS system is a national concern.

**La Bugal-B'laan Tribal Association v. Ramos** (GR 127882, 2004/2005): Mining FTAA in ancestral domain requires FPIC under IPRA; FPIC process must be completed before the ECC is obtained; the ECC does not substitute for FPIC compliance.

**Boracay Foundation Inc. v. Province of Aklan** (GR 197303, 2017): EC for reclamation project in Boracay island; DENR/EMB ECC issuance without proper public participation challenged; SC ordered compliance with EIS public participation requirements.

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — EIS System Consolidated
ECC must be secured BEFORE any earth-disturbing activity or construction; even preliminary site preparation (grading, clearing) without ECC constitutes a violation. The FPIC for indigenous communities under IPRA must precede ECC issuance for projects in ancestral domains — EMB cannot issue ECC without FPIC. ECC conditions are legally binding — breach of ECC conditions is a violation of PD 1586 with criminal consequences. The MMT monitors but cannot order the proponent — only EMB can issue orders. Climate change risk assessment is now a required component of EIS for certain project types — it is not optional.

---

## Special Projects Requiring ECC

**Reclamation projects**: Reclamation of foreshore and other water bodies requires ECC from DENR-EMB; additionally requires approval from the Philippine Reclamation Authority (PRA) and relevant LGU. Reclamation projects are ECPs regardless of size.

**Road and bridge infrastructure**: National road projects require ECC; processing through DENR-EMB central or regional office depending on scale. DPWH is the proponent; environmental safeguard officer designated.

**Real estate development**: Subdivisions and condominiums exceeding threshold size are classified as ECPs or ECA projects; DHSUD registration does not substitute for ECC.

**Tourism facilities in protected areas**: Eco-tourism in NIPAS areas requires ECC; project must be consistent with the protected area management plan; PAWB clearance required before EMB can issue ECC.

---

## ECC and LGU Permitting — Interface

**LGU permits**: Mayor's permit, zoning clearance, building permit — all require ECC as a prerequisite for projects that need one. LGU officials who issue permits without verifying ECC compliance are personally liable.

**Locational clearance**: HLURB (now DHSUD) and LGU zoning clearances must be consistent with ECC conditions — no building permit can override ECC conditions on setbacks, buffer zones, or restricted activities.

**LGU anti-pollution ordinances**: LGUs may adopt stricter environmental standards than the national EIS system requires for local projects; DENR ECC sets the floor, not the ceiling. *Provided*: local standards must be consistent with national environmental framework.

---

## Precautionary Principle in EIS
**R.A. 9729 (Climate Change Act) and Precautionary Principle**: Where there is scientific uncertainty about the nature and extent of potential environmental harm, the burden of proof shifts to the proponent to show the project will not cause significant harm. Applied in EIS review for projects near critical ecosystems, climate-vulnerable areas, and high-biodiversity zones.

**Writ of Kalikasan (A.M. No. 09-6-8-SC, Rules of Procedure for Environmental Cases)**: Extraordinary remedy for persons whose constitutional right to a balanced and healthful ecology is violated or threatened by an unlawful act or omission involving environmental damage of such magnitude that it threatens the life, health, or property of inhabitants in two or more cities/provinces. ECC violation may be the basis for a Writ of Kalikasan petition.

**Writ of Continuing Mandamus**: Court may issue to compel a government agency to perform a duty required by law or order to protect environmental rights; retains jurisdiction to monitor compliance.

## Anti-Hallucination Consolidated — EIS
Never advise a proponent to begin site preparation, earthworks, or preliminary construction before ECC is issued — even pre-construction activities trigger PD 1586 if they constitute environmental disturbance. ECC and FPIC are separate requirements for projects in ancestral domains — DENR/EMB issues ECC; NCIP certifies FPIC; both are prerequisites. Reclamation projects are always ECPs — there is no size threshold below which reclamation is exempt. The Writ of Kalikasan requires damage that threatens life/health/property in two or more cities/provinces — it is not available for purely local environmental damage; the Writ of Continuing Mandamus is the tool for compelling agency enforcement at the local level.

---

## ECC Monitoring and Compliance
**Compliance Monitoring Reports (CMRs)**: Proponents submit semi-annual CMRs to DENR-EMB documenting compliance with ECC conditions. CMRs signed by the duly designated Environmental Compliance Officer (ECO) of the proponent. False CMRs = criminal liability.

**Environmental Compliance Audit (ECA)**: DENR-EMB may conduct periodic audits to verify CMR accuracy; third-party auditors may be required for high-risk projects.

**Grievance mechanism**: Affected communities may raise environmental concerns through the MMT or directly with DENR-EMB; DENR must respond to community complaints within 30 days; unresolved complaints may trigger DENR investigation.

---

## PD 1586 in the Context of Special Laws
**NIPAS Act (R.A. 7586)**: National parks and protected areas have additional environmental restrictions beyond PD 1586; PAWB clearance required; compatible uses only (as defined in protected area management plan). ECC issuable only for compatible uses.

**Fisheries Code (R.A. 8550/10654)**: Fish cage projects in municipal waters, mariculture in Visayas-Mindanao waters — BFAR clearance required in addition to ECC. BFAR and DENR-EMB coordinate for coastal development.

**R.A. 7586 + PD 1586 + IPRA combo**: For projects in protected areas within ancestral domains — three-agency process: PAWB (DENR) for protected area clearance; EMB (DENR) for ECC; NCIP for FPIC. All three required before any project activity commences.
