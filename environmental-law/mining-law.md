# Mining Law (R.A. 7942)

## Primary Sources
**Philippine Mining Act of 1995 (R.A. 7942)** — governs exploration, development, utilization of mineral resources. **DENR-MGB (Mines and Geosciences Bureau)** — primary regulatory authority. **FTAA** — Foreign Technical Assistance Agreement for large-scale mining. **ISAGP and IPRA** — require FPIC for mining in ancestral domains. Load `indigenous-peoples/ipra.md` for IP consultation requirements.

---

## Mineral Resource Rights — Regalian Doctrine
**Constitutional basis (Art. XII §2)**: All lands of the public domain, waters, minerals, coal, petroleum, natural gas, and other natural resources are owned by the State. No private party may claim exclusive ownership of mineral resources.

**Exploration Permit (EP)**: Grants the right to explore a specific area for minerals. Non-exclusive right. Term: 2 years (renewable; maximum cumulative term 6 years).

**Mineral Production Sharing Agreement (MPSA)**: Government enters into MPSA with a qualified contractor to explore, develop, mine, and process minerals. Government receives a share of production. Term: 25 years (renewable for 25 years).

**Financial or Technical Assistance Agreement (FTAA)**: For large-scale mining projects requiring advanced technology and high capital. Open to foreign corporations (100% foreign equity allowed). More comprehensive sharing arrangement.

---

## Environment, Safety, and Indigenous Peoples
**Environmental Work Program (EWP)**: Required for exploration permit holders.

**Environmental Protection and Enhancement Program (EPEP)**: Required for MPSA contractors; includes mine rehabilitation and decommissioning.

**Mining Safety and Health Standards**: DENR-MGB sets occupational health and safety standards for mining operations.

**FPIC (Free, Prior and Informed Consent)**: Required from affected indigenous cultural communities/indigenous peoples before mining operations in ancestral domains. Load `indigenous-peoples/ipra.md` for FPIC procedure.

---

## Small-Scale Mining
Governed by R.A. 7076 (People's Small-Scale Mining Act). Distinguished from large-scale by area, equipment, and capacity. Permits issued by the Provincial Governor through the PMRB (Provincial Mining Regulatory Board).

---

## Prohibited Areas
No mining in: protected areas under the NIPAS Act; buffer zones of watershed reservations; certified ancestral domains without FPIC; all islands ≤ 10 km²; rivers and riverbanks; old-growth forests; proclaimed watershed forest reserves.

---

## Anti-Hallucination Guardrails — Mining Law
Mineral resources are owned by the State under the Regalian Doctrine — no mining right conveys ownership of the minerals until extracted and measured. FTAA is for large-scale mining and allows up to 100% foreign equity; do not apply the 40% foreign equity cap applicable to MPSA. FPIC is required for mining in ancestral domains — absence of FPIC is a ground for suspension/cancellation of the mining right. The constitutional prohibition on small-scale mining by non-citizens does not apply to MPSA or FTAA arrangements. Protected areas under NIPAS are categorically off-limits for mining; no mining permit can be issued regardless of economic interest.

## Mining Permit Types — Detailed

**Exploration Permit (EP)**: Grants exclusive right to explore for minerals. Area: generally 16,200 ha maximum per EP. Holder must: conduct exploration activities within 12 months of grant; submit annual progress report to MGB; file Environmental Work Program (EWP). Cannot transfer EP without MGB approval.

**Mineral Processing Permit (MPP)**: For processing/refining of minerals; separate from MPSA. Requires prior approval of Mineral Processing Program.

**Quarry Permit**: For sand, gravel, clay, guano, and similar quarry resources. Governors issue Quarry Permits (not MGB) for areas within their jurisdiction; subject to DENR-MGB oversight.

**Gratuitous Permit**: For artisanal miners; limited area; no right to sell; for subsistence only.

---

## Environmental Requirements — Mining

**Mine Environmental Protection and Enhancement Program (EPEP)**: Required for MPSA contractors; must address: air quality; water quality; land management; waste management; ecological and biodiversity protection; socioeconomic programs.

**Mine rehabilitation**: Progressive rehabilitation (concurrent with mining); final rehabilitation (upon mine closure). **Mine Safety and Health**: DENR-MGB issues Mine Safety and Health Standards; mandatory compliance; mine safety officers required.

**Environmental Guarantee Fund (EGF) — Mining**: Required deposit equivalent to projected rehabilitation cost; released only after satisfactory site rehabilitation verified by MGB.

**Annual Environmental Protection Expenditure**: Mining contractors required to spend a minimum percentage of their operating costs on environmental protection.

---

## Ancestral Domains and FPIC — Mining Interface

**IPRA requirements (R.A. 8371 §59)**: No person may enter and undertake exploration, development, exploitation, or utilization of natural resources within ancestral domains without a certified free and prior informed consent (FPIC) from the ICC/IP. 

**FPIC process**: (1) NCIP issues certificate of ancestral domain title (CADT) or determines ancestral domain area; (2) applicant notifies NCIP; (3) NCIP convenes ICC/IP consultation; (4) ICC/IP votes through consensus or traditional decision-making; (5) FPIC is granted or withheld.

**Royalty**: ICC/IPs entitled to a royalty of 1% of gross output from mining within ancestral domains.

**Government share from ancestral domain mining**: Mining within ancestral domains — government share reduced; royalty to ICC/IP is additional; DENR coordinates with NCIP.

---

## Regalian Doctrine — Key Principles

**State ownership of minerals**: Art. XII §2 Constitution — minerals are owned by the State even when land is privately titled. Private landowner does not own subsurface minerals. Exception: minerals found in privately-owned land are still owned by the State, but the landowner may have surface rights.

**No self-help exploitation**: Private persons cannot exploit minerals without a mining permit; doing so is illegal quarrying; criminal liability.

**Preferential rights of applicants**: First qualified applicant to file for an EP/MPSA over a given area has priority. "First-in-time, first-in-right" principle for mineral applications.

---

## Social Development and Management Programs (SDMP)

**Requirement for MPSA contractors**: Allocate a minimum of ₱1.50/metric ton of ore mined for social development and management programs benefiting host communities and LGUs.

**SDMP components**: Livelihood programs; infrastructure development; health and education; environmental programs; indigenous peoples' development programs.

**Host community's right to information**: Communities near mining areas have the right to be informed of mining activities and their potential impacts; DENR implements environmental communication programs.

---

## Mining Revenue Sharing

**Government shares under MPSA**: Government receives 60% of net mining revenues (or as negotiated); contractor receives 40%. Additional revenues from excise tax on mineral output (NIRC §151).

**FTAA revenue sharing**: Negotiated individually but must meet minimum government take per DENR-MGB framework. Typically more flexible terms to attract large capital investments.

**Local government share**: Host LGU receives 40% of the national government share of mining revenues (§290 LGC on national wealth sharing).

---

## Key Cases — Mining Law

**La Bugal-B'laan Tribal Association v. Ramos** (GR 127882, 2004/2005): Upheld constitutionality of FTAA in R.A. 7942; foreign participation in mining allowed through service contracts/FTAAs; FPIC required for mining in ancestral domains; the FTAA is a valid mode of State regulation of national patrimony without conveying ownership.

**Republic v. Rosemoor Mining and Development Corp.** (GR 149927, 2002): Mining permit holder does not acquire ownership of mineral lands — the permit grants a right to extract minerals; the State retains the right to manage and recover unpermitted mining activities.

**Celestial Nickel Mining Exploration Corp. v. Macroasia Corp.** (GR 169080, 2009): Priority of EP and MPSA applications — first qualified applicant has priority; conflicting applications from different parties must be resolved based on filing date and qualification.

## Writ of Kalikasan and Mining Operations
**Available against mining operations**: Citizens may petition for the Writ of Kalikasan against mining operations that threaten the life, health, or property of inhabitants in two or more cities/provinces. SC has granted Writs of Kalikasan against mining companies in cases of tailings dam breaches and large-scale environmental damage.

**Writ of Continuing Mandamus vs. DENR**: Courts may compel DENR to enforce R.A. 7942 environmental requirements against non-compliant mining contractors.

**Citizen complaints**: Any person may file a complaint with DENR-MGB for environmental violations by mining contractors; DENR must act within prescribed periods; inaction may be the subject of mandamus.

---

## Mine Waste and Tailings Management
**Tailings dams**: Engineered structures containing liquid mine waste; designed to DENR-MGB safety standards; subject to regular inspection; tailings dam failure is a major environmental risk (e.g., Marcopper disaster 1996; Rapu-Rapu spillages).

**Mine Waste and Tailings Fees**: Mining contractors pay fees into a Mine Rehabilitation Fund (MRF Trust Fund) and a Monitoring Trust Fund; funds available for rehabilitation and monitoring even after mining ceases.

**Acid mine drainage**: Water that becomes acidic when mine waste (pyrite-bearing rock) contacts air and water; long-term post-mining liability; EPEP must address acid rock drainage prediction and management.

---

## Small-Scale Mining Regulation (R.A. 7076)
**Coverage**: Artisanal and small-scale mining operations using simple tools and equipment; limited to 5 hectares per permit; ore processing capacity limited.

**Permit authority**: Provincial Governor issues Small-Scale Mining Permit (SSMP) through the Provincial Mining Regulatory Board (PMRB). DENR-MGB technical assistance required.

**Minahang Bayan**: Designated areas for small-scale mining; DENR-MGB identifies and declares these areas; small-scale miners operate within Minahang Bayan; outside designated areas = illegal.

**Illegal small-scale mining (ISSM)**: Common problem; penalty under R.A. 7076 and R.A. 7942; DENR, PNP, local authorities conduct anti-ISSM operations.

---

## Mining and Taxation
**Excise tax on mineral output (NIRC §151)**: 2% excise tax on actual market value of minerals and mineral products extracted; payable to BIR; 40% share to the host LGU (from national government share via NTA sharing formula).

**FTAA fiscal regime**: Government take negotiated; includes: corporate income tax; excise tax; customs duties; withholding taxes on dividends; FTAA requires minimum government take of X% of cumulative net mining revenues (negotiated; verify specific FTAA terms).

**VAT exemption**: Minerals and mineral products in their original state — generally exempt from VAT (§109 NIRC); however, refined minerals and processed mineral products may be VAT-able depending on the level of processing.

---

## Environmental Legacy of Historical Mining
**Abandoned mines**: Pre-RA 7942 mines often abandoned without rehabilitation; DENR identifies abandoned mines and implements government-funded rehabilitation where no responsible party can be found.

**Marcopper disaster (1996)**: Drainage tunnel breach released copper mine tailings into Boac River, Marinduque; massive ecological damage; criminal prosecution; civil liability; Marinduque designated an environmental damage zone. Key reference for tailings dam safety and mining company liability.

**Rapu-Rapu mine spills (2005–2006)**: Two spills of mine wastewater into Albay Gulf; DENR issued closure order; mining company suspended; environmental investigation ordered.

## Mine Closure and Rehabilitation
**Final Mine Decommissioning Plan (FMDP)**: Required before cessation of operations; must include progressive and final rehabilitation activities, timeline, and funding. DENR-MGB approves FMDP.

**Mine Rehabilitation Fund (MRF)**: Trust fund established for each MPSA/FTAA; contractor deposits rehabilitation costs; fund released only after DENR-MGB certifies completion of rehabilitation. DENR may use fund if contractor fails to rehabilitate.

**Abandoned mine rehabilitation**: Government appropriations (NEDA-approved budgets) for abandoned pre-R.A. 7942 mines where no responsible party can be identified; DAP and DENR implement; Marcopper rehabilitation is the most prominent example.

**Revegetation requirements**: Mined-out areas must be revegetated with suitable indigenous species; success criteria defined in ECC and EPEP; monitored by DENR and MMT for at least 5 years post-closure.
